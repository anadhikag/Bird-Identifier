# Gray Catbird

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Dumetella carolinensis  
Family: Mimidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 22–24 cm  
Average Wingspan: 30–33 cm  
Average Weight: 24–30 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are slightly darker and more uniformly gray; females have paler underparts.  
Distinctive Features: Gray plumage, black cap, and a long, rounded tail.  

---

## Habitat  
Dense thickets, brushy areas, swamps, and forest edges. Prefers habitats with tangled vegetation for nesting and foraging.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across eastern North America, from southern Canada to the Gulf Coast. Winters in the Caribbean, Central America, and northern South America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migration; travels from northern breeding grounds to tropical wintering areas.  

---

## Diet  
Omnivorous, feeding on insects (e.g., beetles, caterpillars), spiders, fruits, and berries.  

---

## Behaviour  
Solitary and territorial during breeding. Known for mimicry, imitating other birds and mechanical sounds. Nests are built in dense shrubs or vines.  

---

## Vocalization  
Produces a distinctive cat-like "mew" call. Songs include a series of whistles, mimicry, and varied notes.  

---

## Breeding  
Builds a cup-shaped nest of twigs and leaves in thickets. Clutch size: 2–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–13 days, shared by both parents.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to deforestation and urban development.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and aids in seed dispersal through fruit consumption.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Mimics over 50 bird species and mechanical sounds.  
- Has a long tail for balance in dense vegetation.  
- Named for its cat-like "mew" vocalization.
