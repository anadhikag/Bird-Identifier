# Arctic Tern

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Sterna paradisaea  
Family: Laridae  
Order: Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 30–33 cm  
Average Wingspan: 74–85 cm  
Average Weight: 85–130 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have slightly darker plumage and more pronounced black caps.  
Distinctive Features: Black cap, red beak, and deeply forked tail; white underparts with a narrow black band on the breast.  

---

## Habitat  
Breeds in Arctic and subarctic coastal tundra, islands, and rocky shores. Non-breeding season spent in open ocean, particularly in Southern Hemisphere waters.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in Arctic regions of North America, Europe, and Asia. Winters in Antarctic waters, with some populations migrating through the Atlantic and Indian Oceans.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Undertakes the longest known bird migration, traveling from Arctic breeding grounds to Antarctic waters and back annually (up to 70,000 km round trip).  

---

## Diet  
Primarily feeds on small fish (e.g., sand eels, herring), crustaceans, and insects. Catches prey by plunge-diving or surface-skimming.  

---

## Behaviour  
Highly social and colonial, nesting in dense groups. Known for acrobatic flight and aggressive defense of nests. Active during both day and night.  

---

## Vocalization  
Calls include loud, shrill cries, sharp whistles, and chattering noises. Vocalizations intensify during breeding season.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests on ground in colonies, laying 2–3 eggs. Both parents incubate for 21–24 days. Chicks fledge after 21–28 days. Breeding season occurs during Arctic summer.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, climate change, pollution, and human disturbance in breeding areas.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates marine invertebrate populations and serves as an indicator species for ocean health. Long-distance migration aids nutrient dispersal across ecosystems.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Undertakes the longest annual migration of any bird species.  
- Lifespan can exceed 30 years, with some individuals living over 40 years.  
- Known for its agility in flight, performing rapid dives and U-turns to catch prey.
