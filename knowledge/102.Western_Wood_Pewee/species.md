# Western Wood Pewee

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Contopus sordidulus  
Family: Tyrannidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 14–15 cm  
Average Wingspan: 23–25 cm  
Average Weight: 14–19 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have slightly darker upperparts; females may appear paler.  
Distinctive Features: Grayish upperparts, white underparts, long thin bill, and a black eye stripe.  

---

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, forest edges, riparian zones, and suburban areas with scattered trees.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across western North America from southern Canada to northern Mexico; winters in Central and northern South America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling to Central America and northern South America for winter.  

---

## Diet  
Insects caught in flight, including flies, beetles, and wasps.  

---

## Behaviour  
Solitary and territorial during breeding; perches on exposed branches to scan for prey.  

---

## Vocalization  
Distinctive "pee-wee" call and a sharp "pip" note.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in trees or shrubs with a cup-shaped nest; clutch size 2–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 13–15 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pesticide use, and climate change impacts on breeding and wintering grounds.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and serves as an indicator species for forest health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Hover-catches insects mid-air, a behavior uncommon among flycatchers.  
- Nests in burned forests during post-fire recovery.  
- Displays a distinctive "wingbeat" pattern during flight.
