# Philadelphia Vireo

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Vireo philadelphicus  
Family: Vireonidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 13–14 cm  
Average Wingspan: 20–23 cm  
Average Weight: 10–13 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a more distinct black eye stripe and brighter yellow underparts; females are slightly duller.  
Distinctive Features: Yellowish-green upperparts, white underparts, black eye stripe, and a white "spectacles" pattern around the eyes.  

---

## Habitat  
Deciduous and mixed forests, especially near water sources such as rivers and swamps.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in eastern North America from southern Canada to the Gulf Coast; winters in Central America and northern South America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Travels from northern breeding grounds to Central America and northern South America for winter.  

---

## Diet  
Insects, particularly caterpillars, beetles, and flies; occasionally consumes berries.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active and agile, flitting through trees and shrubs while foraging. Often sings from exposed perches.  

---

## Vocalization  
A short, musical song consisting of a series of whistles, often described as "philadelphia" or "see-you-see-me."  

---

## Breeding  
Builds a cup-shaped nest in trees, typically in maple or willow. Clutch size is 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, shared by both parents.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pesticide use, and climate change impacts on migratory routes.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and disperses seeds through fruit consumption.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Often nests in the same species of tree where it forages.  
- Its song is used to defend territory and attract mates.  
- Frequently mistaken for the Red-eyed Vireo due to similar plumage.
