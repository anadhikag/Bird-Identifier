# Carolina Wren  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Thryothorus ludovicianus  
Family: Troglodytidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 12–14 cm  
Average Wingspan: 16–18 cm  
Average Weight: 15–18 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Slightly larger males; otherwise similar plumage  
Distinctive Features: Long tail, reddish-brown upperparts, white eyebrow stripe, and a bold white throat.  

---  

## Habitat  
Dense underbrush in woodlands, swamps, thickets, and suburban gardens with ample ground cover.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Native to the southeastern United States (Texas to Florida), expanding northward into New England and southern Canada.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Non-migratory  
Migration Pattern: Residency; remains in range year-round.  

---  

## Diet  
Insects (beetles, caterpillars, spiders), supplemented with berries and seeds in winter.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Highly active, foraging on the ground or in low vegetation. Builds large, domed nests in trees, shrubs, or human-made structures.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Loud, clear, and varied songs including a series of musical notes, mimicry, and a descending "teakettle-teakettle" call.  

---  

## Breeding  
Monogamous pairs; both parents care for young. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation: 12–14 days.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pesticide use, and climate change impacts on insect populations.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and serves as an indicator species for ecosystem health in forested and suburban habitats.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- Expands its range northward due to climate warming and habitat adaptation.  
- Known for its large repertoire of over 100 distinct songs.  
- Frequently nests in human-made structures like birdhouses, mailboxes, or garden gnomes.
