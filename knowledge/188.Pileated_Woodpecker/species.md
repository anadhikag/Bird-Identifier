# Pileated Woodpecker

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Dryocopus pileatus  
Family: Picidae  
Order: Piciformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 16–19 in (40–48 cm)  
Average Wingspan: 26–29 in (66–74 cm)  
Average Weight: 8.8–12.3 oz (250–350 g)  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a red crest; females have a black crest.  
Distinctive Features: Large size, red crest, black and white plumage, white facial stripes.  

---

## Habitat  
Mature forests with large trees, including dead or decaying wood for nesting.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
North America, from southern Canada to the northern United States.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Non-migratory  
Migration Pattern: Unknown  

---

## Diet  
Insects (carpenter ants, beetle larvae), fruits, nuts, and berries.  

---

## Behaviour  
Territorial, drumming on trees to communicate. Excavates large nests in dead trees.  

---

## Vocalization  
Loud, rolling, ringing calls; sharp "kuk" sounds; rhythmic drumming.  

---

## Breeding  
Monogamous pairs. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation: 18–19 days. Both parents care for young.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, logging, and dead wood removal.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Engineers of forest ecosystems by creating cavities used by other species. Controls insect populations.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Largest woodpecker in North America.  
- Uses a specialized "antler" on its beak to drum on trees.  
- Nests are reused by other animals like owls and squirrels.
