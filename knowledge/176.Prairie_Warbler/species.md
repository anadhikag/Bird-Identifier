# Prairie Warbler

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Setophaga discolor  
Family: Parulidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 4.5 inches (11.5 cm)  
Average Wingspan: 7.5 inches (19 cm)  
Average Weight: 0.4 ounces (11 g)  
Male vs Female Differences: Males and females are similar in appearance, though males may exhibit slightly brighter yellow plumage.  
Distinctive Features: Yellow face with black streaks on the sides of the head, white underparts, and a greenish back.  

---

## Habitat  
Prairie Warblers inhabit open woodlands, shrublands, and forest edges, often near water sources. They prefer areas with dense understory vegetation for foraging and nesting.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeding range spans the eastern United States from New York to Florida, extending westward to Texas. Wintering grounds include the Caribbean, northern South America, and parts of Central America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migrants traveling from North America to the Caribbean and northern South America.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily insectivorous, feeding on caterpillars, beetles, spiders, and other small invertebrates. Forages by gleaning insects from low vegetation.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active and agile, Prairie Warblers forage in low shrubs and ground-level vegetation. They are territorial during the breeding season and may engage in displays to defend nesting areas.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song consists of a rapid, high-pitched series of notes: "see-see-see-see" or "tsee-tsee-tsee." The call is a sharp, metallic "chip."  

---

## Breeding  
Nests are built on the ground or in low shrubs, constructed from grasses and leaves. Clutch size is typically 3–4 eggs. Incubation lasts approximately 12 days, primarily by the female.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to deforestation, urbanization, and agricultural expansion.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Prairie Warblers contribute to ecosystem balance by controlling insect populations. Their presence in shrublands indicates healthy, biodiverse habitats.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Prairie Warblers often nest in abandoned ground nests of other birds, such as the Eastern Towhee.  
- They are one of the few warblers that forage extensively on the ground.  
- During migration, they travel at night, using celestial cues for navigation.
