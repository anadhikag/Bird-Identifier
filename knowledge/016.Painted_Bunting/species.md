# Painted Bunting

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Passerina ciris  
Family: Cardinalidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 12–14 cm  
Average Wingspan: 18–20 cm  
Average Weight: 14–19 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are vividly colored with blue, red, and green plumage; females are duller, with greenish-yellow tones.  
Distinctive Features: Males have a striking black eye line and contrasting color patches; females are more cryptic.  

---

## Habitat  
Dense shrubs, thickets, and woodlands near water sources, including suburban gardens and agricultural edges.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in the southeastern United States (Texas, Florida, and the Gulf Coast). Winters in Mexico, Central America, and the Caribbean.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Travels from U.S. breeding grounds to wintering areas in Mexico and Central America.  

---

## Diet  
Insects (especially beetles and caterpillars), seeds, and berries.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and often seen in small flocks outside the breeding season. Males perform acrobatic flight displays during courtship.  

---

## Vocalization  
A high-pitched, melodic warble with varied notes, often delivered from exposed perches.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in dense vegetation, typically thorny shrubs. Clutch size: 2–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, primarily by the female.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, fragmentation, and pesticide use.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and aids in seed dispersal through frugivory.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Males display iridescent plumage to attract mates.  
- Females build nests in thorny shrubs to deter predators.  
- Their vibrant colors derive from carotenoid pigments in their diet.
