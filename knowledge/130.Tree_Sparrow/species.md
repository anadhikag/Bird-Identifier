# Tree Sparrow

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Passer montanus  
Family: Passerellidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 14–16 cm  
Average Wingspan: 20–24 cm  
Average Weight: 20–30 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a black throat and chestnut crown; females are duller with a brownish crown.  
Distinctive Features: Black throat in males, chestnut cap, and a white cheek stripe.  

---

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, farmlands, gardens, and urban areas with trees or nest boxes.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Native to Europe, Asia, and parts of North Africa; introduced to New Zealand and the United Kingdom.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate southward in winter; southern populations are resident.  

---

## Diet  
Omnivorous; feeds on seeds, grains, insects, and human food scraps.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and gregarious, often seen in flocks outside the breeding season. Adaptable to human-modified environments.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A short, melodic series of whistles and trills. Call: Sharp "tsee" alarm call and a soft "chur" contact note.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in tree cavities, buildings, or nest boxes. Clutch size: 3–6 eggs. Incubation period: 12–14 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, agricultural intensification, and pesticide use.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Seeds dispersers and insect controllers in agricultural and urban ecosystems.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- One of the few sparrows that nest in cavities rather than shrubs.  
- Frequently visits bird feeders in urban gardens.  
- Populations have declined in some regions due to habitat fragmentation.
