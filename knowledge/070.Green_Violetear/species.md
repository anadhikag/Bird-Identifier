# Green Violetear

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Colibri thalassinus  
Family: Trochilidae  
Order: Apodiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 10–11 cm  
Average Wingspan: 12–14 cm  
Average Weight: 5–6 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a more extensive violet patch on the head and throat; females have a smaller, less vibrant violet patch.  
Distinctive Features: Iridescent green back, violetear coloration on the sides of the head, and a metallic blue throat in males.  

---

## Habitat  
Montane forests, forest edges, and gardens with flowering plants.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Central America (Mexico to Panama) and northern South America (Colombia, Ecuador, and Venezuela).  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory  
Migration Pattern: Some populations migrate seasonally between highland and lowland regions.  

---

## Diet  
Nectar from flowers, supplemented with small insects and spiders.  

---

## Behaviour  
Territorial around feeding sites; males perform aerial displays during courtship.  

---

## Vocalization  
A high-pitched, metallic "tsik" call and a soft, buzzing hum during flight.  

---

## Breeding  
Females build small, cup-shaped nests from plant fibers and spider silk. Clutch size is typically 1–2 eggs, with incubation lasting 14–17 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to deforestation and agricultural expansion.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Pollinates flowering plants and controls insect populations.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Males defend feeding territories aggressively during breeding season.  
- The species' iridescent plumage is used in courtship displays.  
- Green Violetears hover while feeding, using rapid wingbeats (50–70 per second).
