# Seaside Sparrow

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Ammospiza maritima  
Family: Passerellidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 12–14 cm  
Average Wingspan: 18–21 cm  
Average Weight: 12–18 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a darker, more distinct eye stripe and slightly richer plumage; females are paler with less pronounced streaking.  
Distinctive Features: Dark eye stripe, white throat, and streaked breast; short, conical bill adapted for insect foraging.  

---

## Habitat  
Coastal salt marshes, brackish marshes, mangroves, and tidal flats. Prefers dense vegetation near water for nesting and foraging.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Ranges along the Atlantic and Gulf coasts of the United States, from New Jersey to Texas. Populations are year-round residents in the southern range but partially migratory in the northern range.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory.  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate southward in winter, while southern populations remain resident.  

---

## Diet  
Insects, spiders, seeds, and small invertebrates. Forages by walking or running on the ground, probing mud, or short flights to catch prey.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and often seen in small flocks outside the breeding season. Builds cup-shaped nests in dense vegetation near water. Active during the day, with males singing from elevated perches to defend territory.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A clear, whistled "sweet-sweet-sweet" or "see-you-see-you." Call: A sharp "chip" or "seet" sound.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in dense grasses or shrubs near water. Clutch size: 2–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 11–13 days, primarily by the female. Chicks fledge at 8–10 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to coastal development, sea-level rise, and salt marsh degradation.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and contributes to seed dispersal in coastal ecosystems. Serves as prey for raptors and other predators.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Exists as multiple subspecies with varying plumage, including the "Saltmarsh Sharp-tailed Sparrow," which lacks the dark eye stripe.  
- Nests are often built in mangroves or elevated to avoid flooding.  
- Males perform flight displays during courtship, singing while flying in zigzag patterns.
