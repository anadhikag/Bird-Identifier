# Northern Flicker

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Colaptes auratus  
Family: Picidae  
Order: Piciformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 12–14 inches  
Average Wingspan: 17–21 inches  
Average Weight: 3.5–5 ounces  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a black mustache mark (malar stripe); females lack this feature.  
Distinctive Features: Brown back with black bars, red nape in some subspecies, long pointed bill, and a white rump visible in flight.  

---

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, forest edges, parks, gardens, and agricultural areas. Frequently forages on lawns and in open spaces.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Native to North America, ranging from Alaska and Canada south to Central America. Populations vary by subspecies.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partial migrant  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate to the southern United States, Mexico, and Central America for winter; some populations are year-round residents in milder regions.  

---

## Diet  
Insects (especially ants), beetles, fruits, seeds, and nuts. Forages on the ground using a long, barbed tongue to extract ants from soil.  

---

## Behaviour  
Drums on trees, poles, or metal surfaces to communicate. Social and often seen in pairs or small groups. Excavates nest cavities in dead trees or utility poles.  

---

## Vocalization  
Calls include a loud, ringing "wick-wick-wick" or a sharp "klee-yer". Drumming is used for communication and territory defense.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in tree cavities, laying 4–8 white eggs. Incubation lasts 11–12 days, shared by both parents. Chicks fledge at 25–28 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, competition with invasive species (e.g., European Starlings), and pesticide use.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations, particularly ants. Nest cavities provide shelter for other species like owls, woodpeckers, and small mammals.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Uses its barbed tongue to extract ants from soil, often flicking its head to remove prey.  
- Some subspecies have a red nape patch, while others lack it entirely.  
- The Northern Flicker is the largest woodpecker in North America by length.
