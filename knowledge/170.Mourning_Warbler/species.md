# Mourning Warbler

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Geothlypis philadelphia
Family: Parulidae
Order: Passeriformes

---

## Identification
Average Length: 12–13 cm
Average Wingspan: 18–20 cm
Average Weight: 10–13 g
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a black hood and yellow throat; females have a gray hood and lack the black facial markings.
Distinctive Features: Males have a striking black hood and yellow throat, while both sexes have olive-green upperparts and yellow underparts.

---

## Habitat
Mourning Warblers inhabit dense, low vegetation in forests, particularly in areas with thick understories. They are often found near the forest floor in moist, shaded environments.

---

## Geographic Distribution
Mourning Warblers breed in eastern North America, from southern Canada through the northeastern and central United States. They winter in Central America and northern South America.

---

## Migration
Migratory Status: Migratory
Migration Pattern: They migrate from their breeding grounds in North America to their wintering grounds in Central and northern South America.

---

## Diet
Mourning Warblers primarily feed on insects, including beetles, caterpillars, and spiders. They forage on or near the ground, often in dense undergrowth.

---

## Behaviour
These warblers are generally shy and elusive, often remaining hidden in dense vegetation. They are active foragers, moving through the underbrush in search of food.

---

## Vocalization
The Mourning Warbler's song is a series of short, high-pitched notes, often described as a rapid, chipping sound. Their call is a sharp "tik" or "chit."

---

## Breeding
Mourning Warblers build cup-shaped nests on the ground, hidden among roots and leaf litter. The female lays 3–4 eggs, and both parents share incubation and feeding duties.

---

## Conservation
IUCN Status: Least Concern
Threats: Habitat loss due to deforestation in both breeding and wintering grounds poses a significant threat.

---

## Ecological Importance
As insectivores, Mourning Warblers help control insect populations in their habitats. Their presence also indicates the health of forest understory ecosystems.

---

## Interesting Facts
- Mourning Warblers are often difficult to spot due to their preference for dense undergrowth.
- They are known to use a "leaf-munching" behavior to attract prey.
- The species was named for its somber appearance, particularly the male's black hood.
