# Yellow-breasted Chat  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Icteria virens  
Family: Icteridae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 18–20 cm  
Average Wingspan: 28–31 cm  
Average Weight: 28–35 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a more pronounced black mask and brighter yellow throat; females are duller overall.  
Distinctive Features: Bright yellow breast, black facial mask, and long, graduated tail with white outer feathers.  

---  

## Habitat  
Prefers dense shrubs, thickets, and riparian woodlands, especially near water sources. Avoids open areas.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across central and eastern North America, from southern Canada to the central United States. Winters in Central America and northern South America.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling from breeding grounds to wintering sites in Central America.  

---  

## Diet  
Insects (beetles, caterpillars, grasshoppers), spiders, and small fruits.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Shy and elusive, foraging by hopping through dense vegetation. Often flicks its long tail and mimics other birds’ calls.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of clear, whistled notes, often described as “fee-bee-fee-bee-fee-bee.” Mimics other birds and mechanical sounds.  

---  

## Breeding  
Nests in low shrubs or trees, laying 3–4 pale blue or greenish eggs with brown speckles. Incubation lasts ~12 days, shared by both parents.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss from agricultural expansion, pesticide use, and climate change impacts on wintering grounds.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and serves as an indicator species for riparian ecosystem health.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- One of the largest members of the Icteridae family.  
- Known for its complex, variable song and mimicry of other birds.  
- Often overlooked due to its preference for dense, understory habitats.
