# Fish Crow

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Corvus ossifragus  
Family: Corvidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 43 cm  
Average Wingspan: 81 cm  
Average Weight: 300 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are slightly larger but visually indistinct from females.  
Distinctive Features: Slender build, shorter tail, and a more tapered bill compared to American Crows; plumage with a blue sheen.  

---

## Habitat  
Coastal estuaries, marshes, rivers, lakes, and urban areas near water.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in the southeastern United States from the Carolinas to Florida and west to Texas; winters in the Gulf Coast and northern Mexico.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Short-distance migrants, moving from northern breeding grounds to southern wintering areas.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily carnivorous, feeding on fish, crustaceans, insects, and scavenged food. Also consumes small mammals and human refuse.  

---

## Behaviour  
Highly social, often seen in flocks. Intelligent and tool-using; known to steal food from other birds. Aggressive and vocal, mobbing predators.  

---

## Vocalization  
Distinctive, sharp, metallic "caw" call, higher-pitched than the American Crow's.  

---

## Breeding  
Builds stick nests in trees near water. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation: 18–20 days. Both parents feed nestlings.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pollution, and human disturbance.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates insect and small animal populations. Scavenging helps reduce carrion, preventing disease spread.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Often mistaken for the American Crow due to similar plumage.  
- Known to use tools to extract food from crevices.  
- Fish Crows are more vocal and social than American Crows.
