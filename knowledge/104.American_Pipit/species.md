# American Pipit

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Anthus rubescens  
Family: Passerellidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 14–16 cm  
Average Wingspan: 25–30 cm  
Average Weight: 18–25 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Unknown  
Distinctive Features: Long, slightly notched tail; brown upperparts with dark streaks; pale underparts; white eyebrow stripe; short, straight bill.  

---

## Habitat  
Open habitats including alpine and arctic tundra, grasslands, agricultural fields, and coastal areas. Prefers short vegetation for foraging and nesting.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across North America from Alaska to Newfoundland, extending southward in mountainous regions. Winters in the southern United States, Central America, the Caribbean, and parts of Europe and Asia.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migrants; northern populations travel to temperate and tropical regions for winter. Some populations in milder climates are partially resident.  

---

## Diet  
Insects, spiders, beetles, and other small invertebrates. Occasionally consumes seeds, especially in winter. Forages by running, pausing, and pecking on the ground.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active, ground-foraging birds with a characteristic "bounce-and-pause" movement. Often seen in flocks outside the breeding season. Displays acrobatic flight during courtship.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of high-pitched, musical notes in a descending pattern. Call: Sharp, metallic "pip" or "tsee" sounds.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests on the ground in open areas, often near water. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, shared by both parents. Young fledge after 12–14 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss from agricultural expansion, urban development, and climate change impacts on tundra breeding grounds.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and serves as an indicator species for grassland and tundra health. Seed dispersal through consumption of plant material.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Hover briefly while foraging to inspect prey.  
- Tail flicking is a common display during courtship.  
- Often mistaken for the Tree Pipit but occupies different habitats.
