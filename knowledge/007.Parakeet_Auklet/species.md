# Parakeet Auklet

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Aethia psittacula  
Family: Alcidae  
Order: Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 20–25 cm  
Average Wingspan: 45–50 cm  
Average Weight: 100–150 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Slight size differences; males slightly larger.  
Distinctive Features: Green parrot-like beak with red tip, white and black plumage, yellow wing patch.  

---

## Habitat  
Breeds on rocky coasts and cliffs of islands in the North Pacific. Non-breeding season spent in open ocean.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in the Bering Sea, Aleutian Islands, and Japan. Winters along the coasts of Alaska, California, and Japan.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Moves from northern breeding grounds to southern temperate waters in winter; exact routes poorly documented.  

---

## Diet  
Feeds on small fish (e.g., capelin), crustaceans (e.g., euphausiids), and marine invertebrates. Forages by diving in open water.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and vocal, often seen in flocks. Uses beak to crack shells of prey. Nests in burrows dug into soil or crevices.  

---

## Vocalization  
Emits high-pitched whistles and piping calls, often heard during breeding season.  

---

## Breeding  
Lays one egg per clutch in burrows or crevices. Incubation lasts 21–25 days, shared by both parents. Chicks fledge after 22–25 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat degradation from invasive species, oil spills, and climate change impacts on prey availability.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates populations of small marine organisms. Serves as prey for seabirds, marine mammals, and predatory fish.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Named for its parrot-like beak and colorful plumage.  
- Uses feet to hold eggs while incubating.  
- Pelagic nature makes it difficult to study outside breeding season.
