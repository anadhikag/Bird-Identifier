# Black-footed Albatross

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Phoebastria nigripes  
Family: Diomedeidae  
Order: Procellariiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 85–95 cm  
Average Wingspan: 200–230 cm  
Average Weight: 2.5–4 kg  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are slightly larger and have a more robust bill.  
Distinctive Features: Dark plumage with white underparts, a hooked yellow bill, and long, narrow wings.  

---

## Habitat  
Open ocean habitats, primarily in the North Pacific. Breeds on remote, low-lying islands with sparse vegetation.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in the North Pacific, including Hawaii, Japan, and the Aleutian Islands. Non-breeding ranges extend across the Pacific, with some populations reaching the Southern Hemisphere.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Undertakes long-distance migrations between breeding colonies and foraging grounds, often traveling thousands of kilometers.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily feeds on squid, fish, and marine invertebrates. Scavenges on fish scraps from fishing vessels.  

---

## Behaviour  
Solitary or in small groups. Known for dynamic soaring and slope soaring to conserve energy during flight. Highly social during breeding season.  

---

## Vocalization  
Emits low-pitched, grunting calls during courtship and territorial disputes.  

---

## Breeding  
Forms monogamous pairs. Builds bowl-shaped nests from soil and vegetation. Lays a single egg per clutch. Incubation lasts approximately 65 days, with both parents sharing duties. Chicks fledge after 90–100 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Vulnerable  
Threats: Plastic ingestion, longline fishing bycatch, habitat degradation, and climate change impacts on breeding sites.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates squid populations and serves as an indicator species for ocean health. Scavenging behavior helps reduce marine debris.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Can fly up to 10,000 km without landing during foraging trips.  
- Regurgitates food to feed chicks.  
- Lifespan estimated at 30–50 years.
