# Cape Glossy Starling

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Lamprotornis nitens  
Family: Sturnidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 20–23 cm  
Average Wingspan: 35–40 cm  
Average Weight: 50–65 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males may exhibit slightly more iridescent plumage; otherwise, sexes are similar.  
Distinctive Features: Glossy black plumage with green and purple iridescence, yellow bill, and white wing patch.  

---

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, savannas, riverine forests, and urban areas such as gardens and farmlands.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Southern Africa, including South Africa, Namibia, Botswana, Zimbabwe, Lesotho, and Eswatini.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Non-migratory  
Migration Pattern: N/A  

---

## Diet  
Insects, fruits, seeds, and small invertebrates. Diet varies seasonally; insectivorous in summer, frugivorous in winter.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and gregarious, often seen in flocks. Acrobatic in flight. Known for mimicking calls of other birds and mechanical sounds.  

---

## Vocalization  
Complex vocalizations include whistles, chatters, and mimicry. Mimics include calls of other birds, alarms, and human-made sounds.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in tree cavities, buildings, or nest boxes. Clutch size: 2–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, shared by both parents. Chicks fledge at 21–28 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pesticide use, and competition with invasive species. Urbanization has minimal impact due to adaptability.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and disperses seeds through frugivory. Acts as a bioindicator for ecosystem health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Mimics the calls of other birds and even mechanical noises.  
- Feathers display a metallic sheen due to structural coloration.  
- Often kept in aviculture for its intelligence and vocal mimicry.
