# Eastern Kingbird  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Sayornis torquatus  
Family: Tyrannidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 22–25 cm  
Average Wingspan: 33–38 cm  
Average Weight: 30–45 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Similar in appearance; males may have slightly more pronounced black head plumage.  
Distinctive Features: Black head and upperparts, yellow underparts, and a long, graduated tail.  

---  

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, forest edges, savannas, and suburban areas with scattered trees.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across North America from southern Canada to the northern United States; winters in Central and northern South America.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migrant, traveling from North America to South America for winter.  

---  

## Diet  
Insects, primarily caught in flight (e.g., beetles, flies, wasps). Occasionally consumes small fruits.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Territorial and solitary; males sing from high perches to defend nesting areas. Aggressive toward predators and intruders.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Loud, whistled song consisting of a series of descending notes ("pee-yoo, pee-yoo, pee-yoo"). Calls include sharp, repetitive "pip-pip" sounds.  

---  

## Breeding  
Nests in trees or shrubs, building a cup-shaped nest of twigs, grass, and bark. Clutch size: 3–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 14–16 days, primarily by the female.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to deforestation and pesticide use.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations, particularly pest species. Acts as an indicator species for open woodland health.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- Known to attack larger birds (e.g., hawks) to defend territory.  
- Performs a "tail-wagging" display during courtship.  
- Frequently perches on power lines or fence posts to survey surroundings.
