# Kentucky Warbler

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Geothlypis formosa  
Family: Parulidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 12–13 cm  
Average Wingspan: 18–20 cm  
Average Weight: 10–14 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a more vibrant black face mask and brighter yellow underparts; females are duller overall.  
Distinctive Features: Black face mask extending from eyes to nape, bright yellow underparts, olive-green upperparts.  

---

## Habitat  
Dense undergrowth in deciduous or mixed forests, particularly near the forest floor. Prefers areas with thick vegetation for nesting and foraging.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in the eastern United States (e.g., Ohio, Kentucky, and Tennessee valleys). Winters in Central America, including Mexico, Central America, and the Caribbean.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migrant, traveling from North America to Central America and the Caribbean.  

---

## Diet  
Insects, including caterpillars, beetles, and spiders. Forages on the ground and in low vegetation.  

---

## Behaviour  
Shy and elusive, often remaining hidden in dense foliage. Builds cup-shaped nests on or near the ground, a rare trait among warblers.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of clear, whistled notes resembling "chew-ee-saw." Call: A sharp, high-pitched "tik."  

---

## Breeding  
Nests on the ground or low in vegetation. Clutch size: 3–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, primarily by the female.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss in breeding and wintering grounds, including logging and land conversion.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and serves as an indicator of forest health. Ground-nesting behavior makes it vulnerable to habitat degradation.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Builds nests on the ground, a rare trait among warblers.  
- Relies heavily on caterpillars during the breeding season.  
- Its black face mask is a key field mark for identification.
