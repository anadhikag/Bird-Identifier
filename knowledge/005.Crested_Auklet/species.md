# Crested Auklet

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Aethia cristatella  
Family: Alcidae  
Order: Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 25–30 cm  
Average Wingspan: 50–55 cm  
Average Weight: 200–300 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males may have a slightly larger and more pronounced crest.  
Distinctive Features: Black and white plumage with a prominent white, tufted crest; black face and back; white underparts.  

---

## Habitat  
Breeds on rocky cliffs, islands, and coastal slopes with crevices or burrows for nesting. Forages in coastal and pelagic waters.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in the North Pacific: Alaska (USA), Commander Islands (Russia), and Hokkaido (Japan). Winters in open ocean from the Bering Sea to northern Japan and the Gulf of Alaska.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Travels south from breeding grounds to wintering areas in the North Pacific, often forming large flocks at sea.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily fish (e.g., capelin, herring) and marine invertebrates (e.g., krill, squid). Forages by diving from the surface.  

---

## Behaviour  
Colonial breeder, nesting in dense colonies. Nocturnal on land to avoid predators. Socially vocal during breeding season.  

---

## Vocalization  
Emits a loud, nasal, honking call used for communication in colonies. Sounds are often described as "goose-like."  

---

## Breeding  
Lays one egg per clutch in burrows or crevices. Incubation lasts 28–30 days, shared by both parents. Chicks fledge at 25–30 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat degradation, oil spills, introduced predators on breeding islands, and climate-driven prey scarcity.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates marine invertebrate and fish populations. Nesting colonies contribute nutrients to coastal ecosystems via guano.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Uses its distinctive crest for courtship displays and social signaling.  
- Colonies emit a strong musky odor from preen gland secretions, used in mate selection.  
- Juveniles learn to fly at night to avoid predation.
