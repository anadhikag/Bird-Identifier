# Song Sparrow

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Melospiza melodia  
Family: Passerellidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 14–16 cm  
Average Wingspan: 18–24 cm  
Average Weight: 18–28 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males and females are similar in appearance, though males often have more distinct streaking on the breast and a broader central crown stripe.  
Distinctive Features: Streaked breast, central crown stripe, dark eye line, and a rounded head shape.  

---

## Habitat  
Wetlands, thickets, brushy fields, and forest edges. Prefers dense vegetation near water sources.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
North America, from southern Canada to northern Mexico, and in the Caribbean.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory; some populations migrate, while others are resident.  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate southward in winter; southern populations remain year-round.  

---

## Diet  
Seeds, insects, spiders, and other small invertebrates. Forages on the ground by scratching with both feet.  

---

## Behaviour  
Territorial during breeding season. Males sing repeatedly to defend territory and attract mates. Active during the day.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of short, repeated notes, often described as "Oh-sweet Canada-Canada" or "Merry, merry, merry."  
Call: Sharp "tik" or "chink" sounds.  

---

## Breeding  
Builds a cup-shaped nest in dense shrubs or grass. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, primarily by the female. Both parents feed nestlings.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to urbanization, pesticide use, and climate change.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and aids in seed dispersal. Provides food for predators like raptors and snakes.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Has over 50 recognized subspecies, varying in size and plumage.  
- Adjusts song patterns in noisy environments to avoid masking by human-generated sounds.  
- Uses tools like sticks or leaves to scratch the ground while foraging.
