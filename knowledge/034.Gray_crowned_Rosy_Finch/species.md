# Gray-crowned Rosy Finch

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Leucosticte tephrocotis  
Family: Fringillidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 14–15 cm  
Average Wingspan: 20–22 cm  
Average Weight: 18–22 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have more pronounced rosy tinge on sides and brighter crown; females are duller overall.  
Distinctive Features: Gray crown, pinkish-buff underparts, and rosy flanks; dark eye stripe and white wing patches.  

---

## Habitat  
Alpine tundra, rocky slopes, and high-elevation meadows, often near cliffs or boulders for nesting.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in the Rocky Mountains (USA and Canada), Sierra Nevada, and Cascade Ranges. Winters in lower elevations of the western U.S. and northern Mexico.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partial migrant  
Migration Pattern: Altitudinal migration; breeds at high elevations and descends to lower altitudes or southern regions in winter.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily seeds, supplemented with insects and spiders during the breeding season.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and active; forages in flocks outside the breeding season. Known for acrobatic flight and frequent dust-bathing.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of high-pitched, musical notes. Calls: Sharp, chittering "tsee" sounds and soft trills.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in rock crevices or under boulders. Clutch size: 2–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, with both parents feeding chicks.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss from climate change reducing alpine tundra, and human disturbance in nesting areas.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Seeds dispersers and insect controllers in alpine ecosystems. Their presence indicates healthy high-altitude habitats.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- One of the highest-nesting birds in North America, breeding above 3,000 meters.  
- Forms large winter flocks, sometimes mixing with other finch species.  
- Males display brighter plumage during courtship to attract mates.
