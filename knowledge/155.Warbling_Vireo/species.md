# Warbling Vireo

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Vireo gilvus  
Family: Vireonidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 11.4 cm (4.5 in)  
Average Wingspan: 20 cm (8 in)  
Average Weight: 11 g (0.4 oz)  
Male vs Female Differences: Slight size variation; females may appear slightly duller.  
Distinctive Features: Yellowish belly, white spectacles (eyeliner), and greenish-yellow upperparts.  

---

## Habitat  
Deciduous and mixed forests, especially near water sources.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across eastern North America from Canada to the United States; winters in Central America and northern South America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migrant, crossing the Gulf of Mexico during fall migration.  

---

## Diet  
Insects, including caterpillars, beetles, wasps, and spiders; occasionally consumes berries in winter.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active, agile forager that flits through branches and hovers briefly to catch prey. Often seen hanging upside down while searching for insects.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of short, musical phrases with a whistled "see-saw" pattern. Call: A sharp, high-pitched "see-see-see."  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in trees, constructing a cup-shaped nest of twigs, bark, and spider silk. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation period: 12–14 days, primarily by the female.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss in breeding and wintering grounds, though populations remain stable.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations, particularly in forest ecosystems.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Mimics the songs of other bird species, including woodpeckers and warblers.  
- Frequently hangs upside down to access insects in hard-to-reach crevices.  
- Often mistaken for the Red-eyed Vireo but distinguished by its distinct vocalizations.
