# Indigo Bunting

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Passerina cyanea  
Family: Cardinalidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 12–14 cm  
Average Wingspan: 18–21 cm  
Average Weight: 14–18 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are bright blue with black facial markings and a black V-shape on the chest; females are duller with brown streaks and lack the black markings.  
Distinctive Features: Male’s vivid blue plumage and black facial pattern; both sexes have a notched tail and conical beak.  

---

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, shrublands, forest edges, and weedy fields with scattered trees.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in eastern North America, from southern Canada to the Gulf Coast; winters in Central America, the Caribbean, and northern South America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migrant, traveling from North America to Central America and the Caribbean.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily seeds, supplemented with insects (especially during breeding season).  

---

## Behaviour  
Diurnal and territorial during breeding. Males perform flight displays during courtship.  

---

## Vocalization  
A series of short, musical phrases, often described as a whistled "pink-pink-pink" followed by a trill.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in trees or shrubs; clutch size is 3–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–13 days, primarily by the female.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pesticide use, and collision risks during migration.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Seed dispersal and insect population control.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Males perform courtship flights with rapid ascents and dives.  
- Navigates using star patterns during migration.  
- Blue coloration is structural (light refraction), not pigment-based.
