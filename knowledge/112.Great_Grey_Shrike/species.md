# Great Grey Shrike  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Lanius excubitor  
Family: Laniidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 23–28 cm  
Average Wingspan: 34–38 cm  
Average Weight: 45–65 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a more distinct black mask; females are slightly duller.  
Distinctive Features: Grey plumage, black facial mask, long tail, and sharp hooked beak.  

---  

## Habitat  
Prefers open woodlands, forest edges, shrublands, and farmlands with scattered trees.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across Europe and temperate Asia; winters in sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, and the Middle East.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partial migrant.  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate south for winter; some resident populations in milder regions.  

---  

## Diet  
Insects (especially beetles and grasshoppers), small mammals, birds, and lizards. Impales prey on thorns or barbed wire.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Solitary and territorial during breeding. Perches prominently to scan for prey. Aggressive in defending territory.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Calls include a sharp "tsee" and a varied, melodious song with whistles and trills.  

---  

## Breeding  
Nests in trees or shrubs, building a cup-shaped nest of twigs and grass. Clutch size: 4–6 eggs. Incubation: 14–16 days by the female.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pesticide use, and agricultural intensification.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and preys on small pests. Acts as an indicator species for open woodland health.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- Impales prey on thorns or wires to store food, a behavior called "caching."  
- Known as the "butcher bird" for its hunting and storage habits.  
- Longevity in the wild is up to 8 years.
