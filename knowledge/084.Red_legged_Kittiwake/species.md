# Red-legged Kittiwake

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Rissa tridactyla  
Family: Laridae  
Order: Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 35–40 cm  
Average Wingspan: 85–95 cm  
Average Weight: 300–400 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males slightly larger; both sexes similar in plumage.  
Distinctive Features: Red legs, white underparts, black cap, and pointed wings. Juveniles have brownish upperparts.  

---

## Habitat  
Breeds on rocky cliffs and islands in the Bering Sea and Aleutian Islands. Non-breeding individuals inhabit open ocean and coastal waters.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in the Bering Sea, including the Pribilof and Commander Islands. Winters in the North Pacific, ranging as far south as Japan and California.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Migrates from breeding grounds in the Bering Sea to wintering areas in the North Pacific. Exact routes vary annually.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily feeds on small fish (e.g., capelin, herring) and marine invertebrates (e.g., krill, squid). Forages by plunge-diving or surface-seizing prey.  

---

## Behaviour  
Colonial nester, forming dense breeding colonies. Nests on cliff edges using sticks and seaweed. Active in flocks at sea; performs aerial displays during courtship.  

---

## Vocalization  
Emits high-pitched, piping calls, including a characteristic "kitt-i-wake" sound. Calls are used for communication during breeding and flocking.  

---

## Breeding  
Lays 1–2 eggs per clutch. Incubation lasts 24–26 days, shared by both parents. Chicks fledge after 35–45 days. Breeding success varies with food availability.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Oil spills, climate change, overfishing, and human disturbance at breeding colonies.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Acts as an indicator species for marine ecosystem health. Preys on small fish and invertebrates, influencing local food web dynamics.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Named for its distinctive red legs, a feature absent in the closely related Black-legged Kittiwake.  
- Juveniles take 3–4 years to attain adult plumage.  
- Colonies can number in the thousands during the breeding season.
