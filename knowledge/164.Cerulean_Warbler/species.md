# Cerulean Warbler

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Setophaga cerulea  
Family: Parulidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 11–12 cm  
Average Wingspan: 18–21 cm  
Average Weight: 9–12 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have brighter blue upperparts and a black throat; females are duller with grayish-blue upperparts.  
Distinctive Features: Bright blue upperparts, white underparts, black-and-white wing bars, and a black crown in males.  

---

## Habitat  
Breeds in mature deciduous or mixed forests with dense canopy cover, often near rivers or streams. Wintering habitat includes montane forests in South America.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in eastern North America, from southern Canada to the southeastern United States. Winters in the Andes of Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Travels from North America to South America, crossing the Gulf of Mexico and the Caribbean.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily insects, including caterpillars, beetles, and flies. Forages in the forest canopy.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active and acrobatic, flitting through the canopy. Solitary outside the breeding season.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song is a high-pitched, rapid series of notes: "zee-zee-zee-zee" or "tsee-tsee-tsee," often with a rising inflection.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in the upper canopy of mature trees. Clutch size is 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, primarily by the female. Both parents feed the young.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Near Threatened  
Threats: Habitat loss in breeding and wintering grounds, deforestation, and climate change.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and serves as an indicator species for forest health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Declines of over 70% in North America since the 1970s due to habitat loss.  
- Migrates over 4,000 km annually, relying on specific stopover sites.  
- One of the most vulnerable neotropical migrant birds to climate change.
