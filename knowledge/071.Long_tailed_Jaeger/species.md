# Long-tailed Jaeger

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Stercorarius longicaudus  
Family: Stercorariidae  
Order: Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 38–43 cm  
Average Wingspan: 90–100 cm  
Average Weight: 250–350 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Females slightly larger and more robust; males have longer central tail feathers in breeding plumage.  
Distinctive Features: Long, pointed wings; elongated central tail feathers in breeding adults; dark-backed with pale underparts.  

---

## Habitat  
Breeds in Arctic tundra; winters in coastal and marine environments, including open oceans and rocky shores.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across Arctic regions of Alaska, Canada, Greenland, Scandinavia, and Siberia. Winters along coasts of South America, Africa, and Australia.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance transoceanic migration; travels from Arctic breeding grounds to temperate and tropical coastal zones.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily feeds on fish, crustaceans, and invertebrates. Known for kleptoparasitism, stealing prey from other seabirds.  

---

## Behaviour  
Highly agile and aggressive flier; harasses gulls and terns to steal food. Often seen riding ocean swells or soaring in flocks.  

---

## Vocalization  
Calls include high-pitched "keek" or "kraa" sounds, often used during flight or territorial disputes.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests on ground in tundra, laying 2–3 eggs. Incubation lasts 23–25 days, shared by both parents. Chicks fledge at 21–28 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss from climate change, coastal development, and human disturbance in breeding and wintering areas.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates populations of marine invertebrates and smaller fish. Acts as an indicator species for Arctic and coastal ecosystem health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Known for dramatic "sky dances" during courtship displays.  
- Juveniles resemble adult females but lack the long tail streamers.  
- Frequently follows ships to scavenge food scraps.
