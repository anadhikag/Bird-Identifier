# Yellow-throated Vireo

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Vireo flavifrons  
Family: Vireonidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 4.7 inches (12 cm)  
Average Wingspan: 7.5 inches (19 cm)  
Average Weight: 0.6 ounces (17 grams)  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a brighter yellow throat; otherwise, sexes are similar.  
Distinctive Features: Yellow throat, olive-green back, white underparts, and two white wing bars.  

---

## Habitat  
Inhabits deciduous or mixed forests, often near water sources such as streams or swamps.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across eastern North America, from southern Canada to the Gulf Coast. Winters in Central America and northern South America (e.g., Mexico, Colombia, Ecuador).  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling from North America to Central and northern South America; migrates nocturnally, often along coastal routes.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily insects, including caterpillars, beetles, and flies. Forages in the canopy by gleaning insects from leaves.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active and acrobatic, often hanging upside down while foraging. Territorial during breeding season.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of short, whistled notes: "see-you-see-you-see". Call: Sharp "chip" or "peep" sounds.  

---

## Breeding  
Builds a cup-shaped nest in trees (10–30 feet high). Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation: 13–15 days (shared by both parents). Fledging: 10–12 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss in breeding and wintering grounds; collisions with buildings during migration.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Helps control insect populations, particularly defoliating species.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Often heard before seen due to its loud, distinctive song.  
- Mimics other birds, including woodpeckers and warblers.  
- Named for its prominent yellow throat, though its scientific name ("flavifrons") refers to its yellow forehead.
