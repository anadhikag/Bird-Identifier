# Myrtle Warbler

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Setophaga coronata  
Family: Parulidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 13–14 cm  
Average Wingspan: 20 cm  
Average Weight: 10–14 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a more pronounced black throat and facial markings; females are similar but slightly duller.  
Distinctive Features: Bright yellow rump, white "spectacles" (eyeliner), and a black throat stripe in breeding males.  

---

## Habitat  
Breeds in coniferous and mixed coniferous-deciduous forests; winters in open woodlands, mangroves, and scrub habitats.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across North America from Alaska to Newfoundland; winters in the southeastern U.S., Central America, and the Caribbean.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling from northern breeding grounds to tropical and subtropical wintering ranges.  

---

## Diet  
Insects (especially caterpillars, beetles, and flies) during breeding season; switches to berries and fruits in winter.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active, acrobatic forager, often seen flitting through trees and shrubs. Forms mixed-species flocks during migration.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of high-pitched, whistled notes resembling "tea-kettle-tea-kettle." Call: Sharp, metallic "chip."  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in coniferous trees, building a cup-shaped nest of twigs, moss, and lichen. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–13 days, shared by both parents.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss in wintering and breeding grounds, pesticide use.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and disperses seeds through fruit consumption.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Can digest wax from berries, a rare trait among birds.  
- Historically classified as a subspecies of the Audubon’s Warbler.  
- Uses tail-flicking behavior to communicate during courtship.
