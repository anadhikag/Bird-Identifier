# Ring-billed Gull

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Larus delawarensis  
Family: Laridae  
Order: Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 44–54 cm  
Average Wingspan: 115–138 cm  
Average Weight: 450–600 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Unknown  
Distinctive Features: Black ring around the yellow bill; white head and underparts; gray back and wings; yellow legs. Juveniles have mottled brown plumage.  

---

## Habitat  
Coastal and inland wetlands, including lakes, rivers, estuaries, landfills, parking lots, and agricultural fields.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across North America from Alaska to the northern United States. Winters along the Atlantic, Pacific, and Gulf coasts of the U.S., Mexico, and the Caribbean. Introduced populations exist in Europe and Asia.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate south for winter; some coastal populations remain resident in milder regions.  

---

## Diet  
Omnivorous, feeding on fish, insects, grains, small invertebrates, and human refuse. Scavenges carrion and forages in flocks.  

---

## Behaviour  
Highly social and adaptable. Often seen in flocks near water or human activity. Aggressive at feeding sites; known to steal food from other birds.  

---

## Vocalization  
Loud, raspy "laughing" call, often repeated in a series. Additional vocalizations include grunts and whistles for communication.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests on the ground near water, using sticks, grass, and debris. Clutch size: 2–3 eggs. Incubation lasts 25–29 days, shared by both parents. Chicks fledge at 4–5 weeks.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pollution, and human disturbance. Populations remain stable due to adaptability.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and scavenges carrion, aiding nutrient cycling. Acts as an indicator species for environmental health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Recognizable by the black ring around its yellow bill.  
- Known to live over 30 years in the wild.  
- Frequently observed stealing food from other birds.
