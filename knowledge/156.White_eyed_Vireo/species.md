# White-eyed Vireo

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Vireo griseus  
Family: Vireonidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 13 cm  
Average Wingspan: 23 cm  
Average Weight: 14 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Sexes are similar in appearance; males may appear slightly larger.  
Distinctive Features: White eye ring, olive upperparts, yellow underparts, and a dark line through the eye.  

---

## Habitat  
Dense, thorny thickets, shrubby hedgerows, and overgrown fields. Prefers secondary growth and brushy habitats.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across the eastern United States, from New England to the Gulf Coast. Winters in the Caribbean, Central America, and northern South America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling from North America to the Caribbean and Central America.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily insects, including beetles, caterpillars, and spiders. May consume small fruits in winter.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active and acrobatic, often flitting through dense vegetation. Known for its loud, whistled songs. Territorial during breeding season.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song consists of a repeated series of short, whistled notes: "Oh-oh-oh-oh-oh," with a rising inflection. Calls include a sharp "chip" and a soft "tsee."  

---

## Breeding  
Constructs a cup-shaped nest in thorny shrubs or low trees. Clutch size is 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, primarily by the female. Both parents feed the young.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to land development and pesticide use.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and aids in seed dispersal through fruit consumption.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Mimics the songs of other birds, including warblers and thrushes.  
- Known for its aggressive defense of nesting territories.  
- Often perches upright on thorny branches to avoid predators.
