# Rusty Blackbird

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Euphagus carolinus  
Family: Icteridae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 20–24 cm  
Average Wingspan: 33–38 cm  
Average Weight: 50–70 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are more brightly colored with a rusty body and black head during breeding season; females are duller with less distinct coloration.  
Distinctive Features: Rusty brown body, black head and neck in breeding males, yellow eyes, and a slender, pointed bill.  

---

## Habitat
Breeds in boreal and northern temperate wetlands, including swamps, marshes, and flooded coniferous forests. Winters in flooded agricultural fields, bottomland hardwood forests, and cypress swamps.  

---

## Geographic Distribution
Breeds across Canada and the northern United States, from Alaska to Newfoundland. Winters in the southeastern United States, from Texas to Florida.  

---

## Migration
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Migrates from northern breeding grounds to the southeastern United States for winter.  

---

## Diet
Omnivorous, feeding on insects, spiders, seeds, and aquatic invertebrates. Diet varies seasonally and by habitat.  

---

## Behaviour
Solitary or in small groups during non-breeding season; may form larger flocks in winter. Often forages on the ground or in shallow water.  

---

## Vocalization
Vocalizations include a sharp, metallic "check" call and a soft, musical song with a series of whistles and trills.  

---

## Breeding
Nests in trees or shrubs near water. Clutch size is typically 3–5 eggs. Incubation is performed by the female, lasting approximately 12–14 days.  

---

## Conservation
IUCN Status: Near Threatened  
Threats: Wetland loss, habitat degradation, pollution, and climate change.  

---

## Ecological Importance
Plays a role in insect control and seed dispersal. Indicator species for wetland health.  

---

## Interesting Facts
- Rusty Blackbirds have experienced significant population declines, with some estimates suggesting a loss of over 85% since the 1970s.  
- The species is more commonly observed in winter than during the breeding season due to its remote breeding habitat.  
- The yellow eyes of the Rusty Blackbird are a distinguishing feature not found in other blackbird species.
