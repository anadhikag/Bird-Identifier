# White-crowned Sparrow

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Zonotrichia leucophrys
Family: Passerellidae
Order: Passeriformes

---

## Identification
Average Length: 15–17 cm
Average Wingspan: 23–27 cm
Average Weight: 20–30 g
Male vs Female Differences: Males and females are similar in appearance, though males may have slightly brighter plumage during breeding season.
Distinctive Features: Black and white head stripes, brown-streaked back, and a pale breast.

---

## Habitat
White-crowned Sparrows inhabit a variety of environments, including shrublands, forest edges, and open woodlands. They are often found near water and in areas with dense undergrowth.

---

## Geographic Distribution
White-crowned Sparrows are native to North America. They breed across Alaska and Canada, and are found throughout the United States during migration and winter.

---

## Migration
Migratory Status: Migratory
Migration Pattern: Breeding populations migrate southward to the United States for the winter.

---

## Diet
White-crowned Sparrows are primarily granivorous, feeding on seeds, grains, and insects. Their diet varies seasonally, with more insect consumption during the breeding season.

---

## Behaviour
These sparrows are often seen foraging on the ground in small flocks. They are generally shy and may scatter when disturbed. They are known to visit bird feeders in winter.

---

## Vocalization
The White-crowned Sparrow has a clear, whistled song that varies among subspecies. Their call is a soft "tsee" or "chink."

---

## Breeding
Breeding occurs in spring and early summer. Nests are built on the ground or in low shrubs, and females lay 3–5 eggs per clutch. Incubation lasts approximately 12–14 days.

---

## Conservation
IUCN Status: Least Concern
Threats: Habitat loss due to urban development and agricultural expansion.

---

## Ecological Importance
White-crowned Sparrows contribute to seed dispersal and insect control in their habitats. They are also indicators of ecosystem health in shrubland and forest-edge environments.

---

## Interesting Facts
- White-crowned Sparrows have distinct regional dialects in their songs.
- They are often studied for their role in behavioral and ecological research.
- The species includes several subspecies with varying plumage and vocal patterns.
