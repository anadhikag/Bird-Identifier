# Rock Wren

## Basic Information
Scientific Name:  
*Salpinctes obsoletus*

Family:  
Troglodytidae

Order:  
Passeriformes

---

## Identification
Average Length:  
12–14 cm

Average Wingspan:  
20–24 cm

Average Weight:  
14–19 g

Male vs Female Differences:  
Males and females are similar in appearance, with males often slightly larger.

Distinctive Features:  
Brownish upperparts with dark streaks, pale eyebrow, and a long, thin bill. The tail is long and often flicked.

---

## Habitat
Rock Wren inhabit rocky terrain, including cliffs, talus slopes, and boulder fields, often near streams or in arid regions.

---

## Geographic Distribution
Rock Wren are found across western North America, from southern Canada to northern Mexico.

---

## Migration
Migratory Status:  
Partially migratory

Migration Pattern:  
Northern populations migrate to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico for winter, while southern populations may remain year-round.

---

## Diet
Insects and spiders, including beetles, ants, and flies, which are foraged from rocks and crevices.

---

## Behaviour
Rock Wren are active and often seen bobbing their tails. They are territorial and may build multiple stick nests, using only one for breeding.

---

## Vocalization
The song is a series of clear, whistled notes, often described as musical and flute-like. They also produce sharp calls during flight or when alarmed.

---

## Breeding
Breeding occurs from April to July. Nests are built in rock crevices or under boulders, using twigs, grass, and feathers. Clutch size is typically 3–6 eggs.

---

## Conservation
IUCN Status:  
Least Concern

Threats:  
Habitat loss due to human development and mining in rocky areas.

---

## Ecological Importance
Rock Wren play a role in controlling insect populations and contribute to seed dispersal through their foraging activities.

---

## Interesting Facts
- Rock Wren are known to build multiple stick nests, sometimes in elaborate structures, though only one is used for breeding.
- They are adapted to arid environments and can survive with minimal water.
- The species is often overlooked due to its remote and rocky habitat.
