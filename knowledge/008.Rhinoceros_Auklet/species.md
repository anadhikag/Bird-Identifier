# Rhinoceros Auklet

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Cerorhinca monocerata  
Family: Alcidae  
Order: Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 38–45 cm  
Average Wingspan: 80–90 cm  
Average Weight: 400–600 g  
Male vs Female Differences: No significant differences in plumage; males may have slightly larger bills.  
Distinctive Features: Prominent horn-like structure on the bill, dark plumage with white underparts, and a tufted head.  

---

## Habitat  
Breeds in coastal burrows on rocky islands and cliffs. Non-breeding season spent in open ocean.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
North Pacific Ocean, from Japan and Russia to California, USA.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Moves from breeding grounds in temperate regions to tropical and subtropical waters in winter.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily feeds on fish (e.g., herring, sandlance) and invertebrates (e.g., squid, crustaceans). Forages by diving underwater using wings to swim.  

---

## Behaviour  
Colonial breeder, nesting in dense burrow systems. Nocturnal activity at colonies to avoid predators. Agile swimmers and divers, capable of reaching depths of 60 meters.  

---

## Vocalization  
Produces loud, donkey-like brays and croaks during breeding season. Calls used for pair bonding and territory defense.  

---

## Breeding  
Lays one egg per clutch in burrows. Incubation lasts 50–60 days, shared by both parents. Chicks are brooded for 2–3 weeks before fledging.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat degradation, introduced predators on islands, and overfishing of prey species.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates marine invertebrate populations. Guano enriches coastal ecosystems by depositing nutrients. Indicator species for ocean health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- The "horn" on the bill is a modified upper mandible, more pronounced in males.  
- Capable of diving to depths exceeding 60 meters to catch prey.  
- Known as "devil birds" due to their fierce appearance and vocalizations.
