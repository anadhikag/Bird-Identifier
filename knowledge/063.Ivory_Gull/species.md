# Ivory Gull

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name:  
Pagophila eburnea  
Family:  
Laridae  
Order:  
Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length:  
40–45 cm  
Average Wingspan:  
100–110 cm  
Average Weight:  
300–450 g  
Male vs Female Differences:  
Sexes are monomorphic; no significant size or plumage differences.  
Distinctive Features:  
All-white plumage, black eyes, black-tipped bill, and yellow legs. Juveniles have a dusky mantle.  

---

## Habitat  
Nests on Arctic coastal cliffs, open tundra, and rocky islets. Winters on Arctic and subarctic pack ice or coastal areas.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in Arctic regions of Canada, Greenland, Norway, and Russia. Winters along coasts of Greenland, Norway, and North America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status:  
Migratory  
Migration Pattern:  
Breeding populations migrate south to subarctic or temperate coastal zones; some populations remain in Arctic wintering areas.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily fish (e.g., cod, capelin), marine invertebrates, and scavenges on seal or bird carcasses. Occasionally follows polar bears to scavenge.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and gregarious, often seen in flocks. Flies with slow, deep wingbeats. Follows ships to scavenge food.  

---

## Vocalization  
High-pitched, nasal calls, including a sharp "kraa" or "krrr." Vocalizations increase during breeding season.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in colonies on cliff ledges or rocky slopes. Clutch size: 1–2 eggs. Incubation lasts 28–30 days, shared by both parents. Chicks fledge at 4–5 weeks.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status:  
Near Threatened  
Threats:  
Climate change reducing sea ice, oil spills, human disturbance, and contamination from pollutants.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Acts as a scavenger, cleaning marine environments. Serves as an indicator species for Arctic ecosystem health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- One of the few gulls that breed exclusively in the High Arctic.  
- Named for its ivory-white plumage and pale yellow legs.  
- Capable of locating food in remote, icy environments, often following polar bears.
