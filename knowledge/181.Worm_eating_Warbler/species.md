# Worm-eating Warbler

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Helmitheros vermivorum  
Family: Parulidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 14 cm (5.5 in)  
Average Wingspan: 20 cm (8 in)  
Average Weight: 10–14 g (0.35–0.5 oz)  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have slightly darker plumage and more pronounced black-and-white striping on the sides.  
Distinctive Features: White eye ring, black-and-white striped flanks, and a plain olive-brown back.  

---

## Habitat  
Breeds in dense, deciduous forests with understory vegetation; winters in tropical lowland forests and second-growth habitats.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeding range spans the southeastern and eastern United States; winters in the Caribbean, northern South America, and Central America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Neotropical migrant  
Migration Pattern: Travels from North America to Central/South America, often at night using celestial cues.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily insects, including caterpillars, beetles, and spiders. Forages on the ground and in the understory by flipping leaves.  

---

## Behaviour  
Known for its zigzag, leaf-flipping foraging technique. Shy and elusive, often found in dense undergrowth.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song consists of a series of clear, whistled notes: "wit-wit-wit-wit" or "fee-bee-fee-bee." Calls are sharp and high-pitched.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests on the ground in dense vegetation. Clutch size: 3–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–13 days, shared by both parents.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to deforestation and fragmentation in breeding and wintering ranges.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and contributes to seed dispersal through frugivory during migration.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- The only warbler in North America that nests exclusively on the ground.  
- Males perform a "flight song" during courtship, singing while flying in a zigzag pattern.  
- Its leaf-flipping foraging behavior is unique among warblers.
