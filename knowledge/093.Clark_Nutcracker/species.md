# Clark's Nutcracker

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Nucifraga columbiana  
Family: Corvidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 25–30 cm  
Average Wingspan: 40–45 cm  
Average Weight: 90–110 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Sexes are similar in appearance, though males may be slightly larger.  
Distinctive Features: Black head, white cheeks, and a long, graduated tail. Bill is robust and adapted for extracting pine seeds.  

---

## Habitat  
Clark's Nutcrackers inhabit coniferous forests, particularly those dominated by pine species. They prefer high-elevation habitats in mountainous regions.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Native to North America, ranging from Alaska and western Canada to the western United States (e.g., New Mexico).  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory  
Migration Pattern: Some populations migrate to lower elevations in winter, while others remain in their range year-round.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily consumes pine seeds, which they cache in thousands of underground storage sites. Also eats insects, berries, and other plant matter.  

---

## Behaviour  
Clark's Nutcrackers are highly social and intelligent, using spatial memory to retrieve cached seeds. They play a critical role in seed dispersal for pine species.  

---

## Vocalization  
Their calls include a loud, nasal "yank-yank" similar to jays, as well as a variety of chittering and alarm calls.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests are built in conifer trees using twigs and bark strips. Clutch size is typically 2–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 18–20 days, with both parents sharing care.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to logging and climate change impacts on pine forests.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Clark's Nutcrackers are keystone species for pine regeneration, as they disperse seeds from over 50 pine species.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- They can remember the locations of thousands of seed caches.  
- Their mutualistic relationship with pines aids in forest regeneration.  
- The species has a specialized bill with a "nutcracker" mechanism for extracting seeds.
