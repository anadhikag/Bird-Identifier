# Tennessee Warbler  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Geothlypis trichas  
Family: Parulidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 11 cm  
Average Wingspan: 18 cm  
Average Weight: 10 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a brighter yellow crown and more distinct black eye stripe; females are duller overall.  
Distinctive Features: Yellow crown, black eye stripe, olive upperparts, and pale underparts.  

---  

## Habitat  
Breeds in deciduous and mixed forests with dense understory, often near water. Winters in tropical forests and second-growth habitats in Central America.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across eastern North America from Canada to the northeastern United States. Winters in Central America, including Costa Rica, Panama, and northern South America.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Long-distance migrant.  
Migration Pattern: Travels from breeding grounds to Central America, crossing the Gulf of Mexico.  

---  

## Diet  
Primarily insects, including caterpillars, beetles, and flies. Occasionally consumes berries.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Active and agile, flitting through foliage while foraging. Often joins mixed-species flocks during migration. Shy and elusive, preferring dense cover.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Song: A rapid, high-pitched series of notes: *tsee-tsee-tsee*, often ending with a trill. Call: A sharp *chip*.  

---  

## Breeding  
Nests in low shrubs or on the ground in dense vegetation. Clutch size: 3–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, primarily by the female.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss in breeding and wintering ranges, pesticide use.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations, particularly caterpillars, in both breeding and wintering habitats. Acts as a seed disperser for some plant species.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- Migrates over 5,000 km annually between North and Central America.  
- Feeds almost exclusively on insects during the breeding season.  
- Plays a role in controlling forest pest populations.
