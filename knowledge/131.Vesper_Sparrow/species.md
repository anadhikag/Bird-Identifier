# Vesper Sparrow

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Passerculus sandwichensis  
Family: Passerellidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 13–14 cm  
Average Wingspan: 19–22 cm  
Average Weight: 14–20 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a more distinct black eye stripe and a faint breast spot; females are slightly duller.  
Distinctive Features: Black eye stripe, white wing patch, and a short, notched tail.  

---

## Habitat  
Open grasslands, prairies, and agricultural fields with sparse vegetation.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across central North America, from southern Canada to the central United States. Winters in the southern United States, northern Mexico, and the Caribbean.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling from breeding grounds in North America to wintering areas in the southern U.S. and Mexico.  

---

## Diet  
Seeds and insects; forages on the ground by scratching vegetation.  

---

## Behaviour  
Solitary or in small flocks; builds nests on the ground using grasses and twigs. Active during the day.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of clear, whistled notes ("ves-per-spar-row"), often delivered from a perch.  

---

## Breeding  
Lays 3–5 eggs in a ground nest; incubation lasts 11–13 days. Both parents care for young.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to agricultural expansion and urbanization.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Seeds and insect population control; serves as prey for larger predators.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Prefers habitats with low vegetation for nesting and foraging.  
- Known for its melodic, whistled song often heard at dusk.  
- Ground-nesting behavior makes it vulnerable to predation and habitat disturbance.
