# European Goldfinch

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Carduelis carduelis  
Family: Fringillidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 11–12 cm  
Average Wingspan: 20–23 cm  
Average Weight: 11–18 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have brighter red and yellow plumage; females are slightly duller.  
Distinctive Features: Black and white face markings, red face, yellow wings with a black bar, and a short, conical beak.  

---

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, gardens, parks, farmlands, and scrublands. Often found near human settlements.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Widespread across Europe, North Africa, and parts of Western Asia. Resident in southern populations; northern populations migrate.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate to southern Europe and North Africa in winter; southern populations are resident.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily seeds (e.g., thistle, teasel), supplemented with insects (especially during breeding season). Uses its beak to crack open seeds.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active and acrobatic, often hanging upside down on plants to feed. Social, forming small flocks outside the breeding season.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: Varied, musical warble with trills and whistles. Calls: Sharp "chi-weet" and a "tsee-tsee" alarm call.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in trees or shrubs, building a cup-shaped nest. Clutch size: 4–6 eggs. Incubation: 12–14 days (mainly by the female). Both parents feed the young. 1–2 broods per year.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, agricultural intensification, and pesticide use.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Seeds dispersers; help control insect populations. Indicator species for ecosystem health in open habitats.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Known for hanging upside down to feed on seeds.  
- Uses its beak to crack open tough seed shells.  
- Often visits garden feeders, especially in winter.
