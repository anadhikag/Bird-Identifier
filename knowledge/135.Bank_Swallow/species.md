# Bank Swallow

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Riparia riparia  
Family: Hirundinidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 12–14 cm  
Average Wingspan: 25–28 cm  
Average Weight: 14–20 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have slightly darker throat plumage; otherwise, sexes are similar.  
Distinctive Features: Dark upperparts, pale underparts, long forked tail, and a small, pointed beak.  

---

## Habitat  
Open habitats with exposed soil for nesting, including riverbanks, cliffs, gravel pits, and agricultural fields. Often found near water bodies.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across North America, Europe, and parts of Asia; winters in South America, sub-Saharan Africa, and southern Asia.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling from northern breeding grounds to tropical regions in the southern hemisphere.  

---

## Diet  
Insects, primarily caught in flight. Feeds on flies, beetles, mosquitoes, and other flying insects.  

---

## Behaviour  
Highly social, often seen in flocks. Flies low and fast, hawking insects mid-air. Nests in colonies in burrows dug into vertical banks or cliffs.  

---

## Vocalization  
Soft, high-pitched calls including a sharp "tsee" or "twee." Chattering and trilling sounds are common in flocks.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in burrows up to 2 m deep in banks or cliffs. Lays 4–6 white eggs with brown speckles. Incubation lasts 14–16 days, shared by both parents.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss from human development, nest site destruction, and pesticide use reducing insect prey.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations, particularly pests. Indicator species for open habitat health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Migrates over 10,000 km annually in some populations.  
- Roosts in massive flocks of thousands during winter.  
- Nests are often reused by multiple generations and other species.
