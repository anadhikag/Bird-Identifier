# Vermilion Flycatcher

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Pyrocephalus rubinus  
Family: Tyrannidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 13–15 cm  
Average Wingspan: 20–23 cm  
Average Weight: 12–18 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a bright red head, throat, and breast; females are grayish-brown with a pale belly.  
Distinctive Features: Males display a striking red crown and face; females have a white eye ring and pale underparts.  

---

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, savannas, riparian zones, and scrublands. Prefers areas with sparse vegetation and perches for insect hunting.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Native to the Americas, ranging from the southwestern United States (Arizona, New Mexico) through Central America to northern Argentina.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Breeds in North America; winters in Central and South America.  

---

## Diet  
Insects, including beetles, grasshoppers, and flying ants, caught in mid-air. Occasionally consumes small fruits.  

---

## Behaviour  
Territorial during breeding season. Males perform aerial displays to defend territory and attract mates. Active during the day, often seen perched conspicuously.  

---

## Vocalization  
A sharp, whistled "pip-pip-pip" call and a melodic, descending trill during courtship.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in trees or shrubs, building a cup-shaped nest from twigs, grass, and spider silk. Clutch size: 2–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 14–16 days, shared by both parents.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss from agricultural expansion, urbanization, and pesticide use.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations, particularly pests in agricultural areas. Indicator species for healthy riparian ecosystems.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Males perform U-shaped courtship flights to showcase their red plumage.  
- Nests are often placed in thorny vegetation to deter predators.  
- Known as "Red-crested Flycatcher" in some regions due to its distinctive head coloration.
