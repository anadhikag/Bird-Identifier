# Shiny Cowbird

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Molothrus bonariensis  
Family: Icteridae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 18–20 cm  
Average Wingspan: 30–35 cm  
Average Weight: 30–40 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are uniformly glossy black with a metallic sheen; females are duller with brownish plumage and faint streaking.  
Distinctive Features: Iridescent black plumage, slender bill, and a sharp, metallic call.  

---

## Habitat  
Open grasslands, agricultural fields, savannas, and urban areas. Often found near livestock.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Native to South America, ranging from central Argentina to northern Brazil, with isolated populations in the southern United States.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory.  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate seasonally; southern populations are largely sedentary.  

---

## Diet  
Insects (especially grasshoppers, beetles), seeds, grains, and occasionally small vertebrates.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and nomadic; often follows cattle to catch insects disturbed by their movement. Known for brood parasitism.  

---

## Vocalization  
Short, sharp "click" calls and a series of high-pitched, metallic "tik-tik-tik" notes used for communication and territorial defense.  

---

## Breeding  
Lays eggs in the nests of other bird species (brood parasitism). Hosts include finches, sparrows, and warblers. No parental care provided.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss and pesticide use in agricultural areas.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and disperses seeds. Brood parasitism influences host species' reproductive strategies.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Lays eggs in over 200 host species' nests, often evicting host eggs.  
- Follows livestock to feed on insects and larvae stirred up by their movement.  
- Males perform aerial displays during the breeding season to attract females.
