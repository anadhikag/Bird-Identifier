# Western Grebe

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Aechmophorus occidentalis  
Family: Podicipedidae  
Order: Podicipediformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 60–75 cm  
Average Wingspan: 100–120 cm  
Average Weight: 1.1–1.5 kg  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are slightly larger and have longer necks; otherwise, plumage is similar.  
Distinctive Features: Black-and-white plumage, long slender neck, black eye stripe, and a small, sharp bill.  

---

## Habitat  
Freshwater lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers during breeding; coastal marine environments in winter.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in western North America, from Alaska to northern California; winters along the Pacific coast from California to Baja California.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Migrates southward from northern breeding grounds to coastal wintering areas.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily fish, supplemented with aquatic insects, crustaceans, and amphibians. Uses its long neck to spear prey underwater.  

---

## Behaviour  
Swims and dives with agility; performs elaborate courtship displays. Often seen in pairs or small groups.  

---

## Vocalization  
Emits low, frog-like croaks and grunts, often during courtship or territorial disputes.  

---

## Breeding  
Builds floating nests of aquatic vegetation anchored to emergent plants. Lays 2–5 eggs per clutch; both parents incubate for 23–25 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to wetland drainage, pollution, and human disturbance.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls fish populations and serves as an indicator species for wetland health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Performs a synchronized courtship display called the "rushing display," where pairs run across water in unison.  
- Juveniles are born with dark, mottled plumage and lack the adult's distinctive eye stripe.  
- Known for its ability to dive deeply, reaching up to 10 meters in search of prey.
