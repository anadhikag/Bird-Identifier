# Caspian Tern

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Hydroprogne caspia  
Family: Sternidae  
Order: Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 43–48 cm  
Average Wingspan: 100–115 cm  
Average Weight: 250–350 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Sexes are similar; males may be slightly larger.  
Distinctive Features: Black cap, white cheeks, long heavy bill, and a streamlined body.  

---

## Habitat  
Coastal and inland water bodies, including lakes, rivers, estuaries, and marine coasts. Nests on sandbars, islands, or man-made structures.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across Europe, Asia, and North America. Winters in Africa, southern Asia, the Caribbean, and along tropical coasts.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migrants; some populations travel to sub-Saharan Africa or southern South America.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily fish, caught through plunge diving. Also consumes crustaceans, insects, and small amphibians.  

---

## Behaviour  
Colonial nester with aerial courtship displays. Aggressive toward intruders near nests. Often forages in groups.  

---

## Vocalization  
Harsh, croaking calls; loud, grating "kraa" sounds during breeding season.  

---

## Breeding  
Lays 2–3 eggs in a ground scrape or on elevated nests. Incubation lasts 25–28 days. Both parents feed chicks.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, human disturbance, and pollution.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates fish populations and serves as an indicator species for wetland health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- The largest tern species by weight.  
- Known for its dramatic plunge-diving technique to catch fish.  
- Some populations nest on offshore oil platforms and breakwaters.
