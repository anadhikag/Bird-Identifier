# Common Tern

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Sterna hirundo  
Family: Laridae  
Order: Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 30–34 cm  
Average Wingspan: 70–75 cm  
Average Weight: 100–150 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are slightly larger and have a more pronounced black cap.  
Distinctive Features: Black cap, white forehead, long pointed bill, and streamlined body with narrow wings.  

---

## Habitat  
Coastal areas, sandy or gravel beaches, estuaries, and inland lakes or rivers with open water.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across Europe, Asia, North America, and parts of Africa; winters in tropical and subtropical coasts.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migrants; some populations travel thousands of kilometers to tropical regions.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily small fish, caught by plunge-diving; also eats insects, crustaceans, and amphibians.  

---

## Behaviour  
Colonial nester; agile in flight, often hovering before diving for prey.  

---

## Vocalization  
High-pitched, sharp calls; common sounds include a shrill "kriek" and softer "peep" during courtship.  

---

## Breeding  
Lays 2–3 eggs in a ground scrape or on floating nests; both parents incubate for 21–25 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, human disturbance, and pollution.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls fish populations and serves as an indicator species for coastal ecosystem health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Can dive from heights of up to 10 meters to catch fish.  
- Forms large nesting colonies, sometimes with other tern species.  
- Longevity in the wild is up to 30 years.
