# Red-headed Woodpecker

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Melanerpes erythrocephalus  
Family: Picidae  
Order: Piciformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 20–24 cm  
Average Wingspan: 35–40 cm  
Average Weight: 70–100 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a red nape; females lack this feature.  
Distinctive Features: Bright red head, black and white body plumage, and a long, chisel-like beak.  

---

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, forest edges, orchards, and parks with dead trees for nesting.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Native to North America, ranging from southern Canada through the central and eastern United States.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory.  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate to the southeastern U.S. in winter; southern populations may remain year-round.  

---

## Diet  
Insects (especially beetles), fruits, nuts, acorns, and occasionally small vertebrates or bird eggs.  

---

## Behaviour  
Territorial and solitary, often seen perched on dead trees. Stores food in bark crevices and excavates nest cavities.  

---

## Vocalization  
Calls include a sharp "kik" and a rolling "krarrr." Drumming is used for communication and territory defense.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in cavities of dead or dying trees. Clutch size is 4–5 eggs, incubated by both parents for 14–16 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to forest clearing and pesticide use.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and creates cavities used by other species. Disperses seeds through fruit consumption.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Stores food in tree bark crevices for later retrieval.  
- One of the few woodpeckers that hovers briefly to catch insects mid-air.  
- Known to eat venomous snakes, such as cottonmouths.
