# Forster's Tern

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Sterna forsteri  
Family: Laridae  
Order: Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 30–34 cm  
Average Wingspan: 66–75 cm  
Average Weight: 110–150 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males slightly larger with darker plumage; females may have paler underparts.  
Distinctive Features: Black cap, white underparts, deeply forked tail, and a slender, pointed bill.  

---

## Habitat  
Coastal beaches, estuaries, inland lakes, and riverbanks. Prefers open water with accessible nesting sites.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across North America, Europe, and Asia. Winters in tropical and subtropical regions, including South America, Africa, and Southeast Asia.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate to southern hemisphere coasts; North American birds winter in South America.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily small fish, caught via plunge diving. Also consumes insects, crustaceans, and amphibians.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and gregarious; nests in colonies. Agile flier, often hovers before diving for prey. Aggressive during territorial disputes.  

---

## Vocalization  
Calls include sharp, high-pitched "peeep" and "krrr" sounds. Noisy in colonies.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests on the ground near water, often on sand or gravel. Clutch size: 2–4 eggs. Incubation: 21–25 days. Both parents feed chicks.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, coastal development, pollution, and human disturbance.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates fish and invertebrate populations. Indicator species for coastal ecosystem health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Hovers briefly before plunge-diving for fish.  
- Nests in dense colonies to deter predators.  
- Capable of catching fish mid-air during flight.
