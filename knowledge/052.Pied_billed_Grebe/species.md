# Pied Billed Grebe

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Podilymbus podiceps  
Family: Podicipedidae  
Order: Podicipediformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 30–38 cm  
Average Wingspan: 46–58 cm  
Average Weight: 200–400 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Sexes are similar in appearance; males may be slightly larger.  
Distinctive Features: Black-and-white striped bill, tufted head, and ability to submerge rapidly when threatened.  

---

## Habitat  
Inhabits freshwater lakes, ponds, marshes, and slow-moving rivers with dense vegetation. Occasionally found in brackish coastal lagoons.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across North America from southern Canada to northern Mexico; winters in the southern U.S., Central America, and northern South America. Populations in the Caribbean and South America are resident year-round.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory.  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate southward for winter; some tropical populations remain resident.  

---

## Diet  
Feeds on aquatic insects, small fish, crustaceans, amphibians, and aquatic plants. Forages by diving and walking on the waterbed.  

---

## Behaviour  
Solitary or in pairs. Nests in floating vegetation. Known for its ability to sink rapidly to avoid predators. Displays elaborate courtship rituals involving synchronized swimming.  

---

## Vocalization  
Emits low, grating croaks and whistles. Vocalizations are frequent during breeding season.  

---

## Breeding  
Builds floating nests anchored to vegetation. Clutch size: 2–6 eggs. Both parents incubate for 23–28 days and care for chicks, which ride on parents’ backs.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to wetland drainage, water pollution, and human disturbance.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls populations of aquatic invertebrates and small fish. Serves as an indicator species for wetland health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Can adjust buoyancy by expelling air from feathers to submerge quickly.  
- Chicks are precocial and can swim within hours of hatching.  
- Builds floating nests using aquatic plants, which rise and fall with water levels.
