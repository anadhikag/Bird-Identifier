# Savannah Sparrow

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Passerculus sandwichensis  
Family: Passerellidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 14–16 cm  
Average Wingspan: 18–22 cm  
Average Weight: 18–25 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males and females are similar in appearance, though males may exhibit slightly more pronounced breast streaking.  
Distinctive Features: Dark eye stripe, white throat, and a buffy crown.  

---

## Habitat  
Open grasslands, meadows, savannas, and agricultural fields. Prefers areas with low vegetation for nesting and foraging.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across North America from southern Canada to the northern United States. Winters in the southern U.S., Mexico, and parts of Central America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partial migrant.  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate southward in winter; southern populations may remain resident.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily seeds and insects. Forages on the ground, gleaning seeds or catching insects.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and often found in flocks outside the breeding season. Nests on the ground in dense grass.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A clear, whistled "pip-pip-pip-pip-pip" with a rising inflection. Call: A sharp, high-pitched "tik."  

---

## Breeding  
Nests on the ground in grassy areas. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12 days, with both parents sharing duties.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to agricultural expansion, urban development, and grassland conversion.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Seeds and insect dispersers; helps maintain grassland ecosystem balance.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Exhibits regional plumage variation among subspecies.  
- Often nests in agricultural fields but relies on native grasslands for breeding.  
- Known for its distinctive, melodic song pattern.
