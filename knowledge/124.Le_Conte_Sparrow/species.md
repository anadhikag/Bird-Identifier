# Le Conte's Sparrow

## Basic Information
Scientific Name:  
Ammodramus leconteii  
Family:  
Passerellidae  
Order:  
Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length:  
13 cm  
Average Wingspan:  
19 cm  
Average Weight:  
15 g  
Male vs Female Differences:  
Unknown  
Distinctive Features:  
Small size, buffy crown, dark eye stripe, and a short, notched tail.  

---

## Habitat
Le Conte's Sparrow inhabits dense, weedy grasslands, including agricultural fields, roadsides, and overgrown pastures. It prefers habitats with tall vegetation and limited human disturbance.  

---

## Geographic Distribution
This species is found across central North America, from southern Canada through the central United States. It is most commonly observed in the Great Plains region.  

---

## Migration
Migratory Status:  
Partially migratory  
Migration Pattern:  
Northern populations migrate to the southern United States and northern Mexico for the winter, while some southern populations remain resident year-round.  

---

## Diet
Le Conte's Sparrow primarily feeds on seeds, supplemented with insects during the breeding season.  

---

## Behaviour
This sparrow is secretive and often difficult to observe due to its dense habitat preference. It is generally solitary outside the breeding season.  

---

## Vocalization
The song is a short, buzzy trill, often described as a rapid "chirr" or "zzzz." Calls are soft and high-pitched.  

---

## Breeding
Breeding occurs in late spring and early summer. Nests are built on the ground in dense vegetation. Clutch size is typically 3–4 eggs, with incubation lasting approximately 11–13 days.  

---

## Conservation
IUCN Status:  
Least Concern  
Threats:  
Habitat loss due to agricultural expansion and urban development.  

---

## Ecological Importance
As a seed-eater and insectivore, Le Conte's Sparrow contributes to seed dispersal and insect population control in its grassland habitats.  

---

## Interesting Facts
- Le Conte's Sparrow is one of the most difficult sparrows to identify due to its similarity with other small sparrow species.  
- It is named after John Le Conte, a 19th-century American entomologist.  
- The species is often overlooked due to its secretive nature and habitat preference.
