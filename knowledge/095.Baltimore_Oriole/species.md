# Baltimore Oriole

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Icterus galbula  
Family: Icteridae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 17–21 cm  
Average Wingspan: 25–30 cm  
Average Weight: 17–30 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are brightly colored with black and orange; females are duller with yellowish-orange and gray.  
Distinctive Features: Bright black and orange plumage in males, with a distinct white wing bar and black face.  

---

## Habitat
Baltimore Orioles inhabit open woodlands, forest edges, and suburban areas with mature trees. They are often found near water sources and in orchards or parks with suitable nesting sites.

---

## Geographic Distribution
Baltimore Orioles are found across eastern North America, from southern Canada to the Gulf Coast, and as far west as the Great Plains. They also winter in Central America and northern South America.

---

## Migration
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Breeds in North America and winters in Central and northern South America. They migrate in flocks, often using coastal flyways.

---

## Diet
Baltimore Orioles are primarily insectivorous during the breeding season, feeding on caterpillars, beetles, and other insects. In winter, they switch to a diet of fruits and nectar.

---

## Behaviour
These birds are active and often seen flitting through trees. They are territorial during the breeding season and may aggressively defend feeding areas. They are also known to visit backyard feeders for jelly and oranges.

---

## Vocalization
Baltimore Orioles have a clear, flute-like song consisting of a series of whistles and warbles. Their calls are sharp and include a distinctive "pip-pip-pip" sound.

---

## Breeding
Baltimore Orioles build hanging, woven nests in trees, often near water. Females lay 3–7 eggs per clutch, and both parents share incubation and feeding duties. The young fledge after about 12–14 days.

---

## Conservation
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to deforestation and urban development, as well as pesticide use affecting insect populations.

---

## Ecological Importance
Baltimore Orioles play a role in controlling insect populations during the breeding season and help in seed dispersal during their winter fruit consumption.

---

## Interesting Facts
- Baltimore Orioles are known to use human-made structures, such as clotheslines and mist nets, for nesting.
- They are often attracted to bright orange and red colors, which they use to identify ripe fruits.
- The male's vibrant plumage is used to attract mates and defend territory.
