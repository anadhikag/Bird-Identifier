# Harris's Sparrow

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Zonotrichia querula  
Family: Passerellidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 16–18 cm  
Average Wingspan: 25–28 cm  
Average Weight: 24–30 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a black hood, throat, and breast; females are more subdued with less distinct black markings.  
Distinctive Features: Black head and breast in males, white belly, and a sparrow-like body with a long tail.  

---

## Habitat  
Breeds in open grasslands, shrublands, and agricultural areas in central Canada. Winters in open habitats, including grasslands and weedy fields, in the southern United States.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeding range spans central Canada (Saskatchewan to Manitoba). Wintering range extends to the southern U.S. (Texas, Oklahoma, and New Mexico).  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling from Canada to the southern U.S.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily granivorous, feeding on seeds. Insects and other arthropods are also consumed, especially during the breeding season.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social birds that forage on the ground in flocks outside the breeding season. Males perform courtship displays involving wing-fluttering and tail-fanning.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song consists of a series of clear, whistled notes, often described as a musical trill. Calls include sharp, high-pitched "tsee" sounds.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests are built on the ground in grassy or shrubby areas. Clutch size is 3–5 eggs, with incubation lasting approximately 12–14 days. Both parents care for the young.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to agricultural expansion and climate change affecting grassland ecosystems.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Plays a role in seed dispersal and insect population control. Serves as prey for larger predators in its ecosystem.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- The only sparrow in North America with a distinctive black hood and breast.  
- Males perform elaborate courtship displays to attract mates.  
- Known for its long-distance migration, traveling over 2,000 km annually.
