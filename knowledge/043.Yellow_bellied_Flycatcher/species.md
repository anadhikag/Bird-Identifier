# Yellow-bellied Flycatcher

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Empidonax flaviventris  
Family: Tyrannidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 13–14 cm  
Average Wingspan: 20–23 cm  
Average Weight: 11–14 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males may be slightly larger and have brighter plumage than females.  
Distinctive Features: Yellow belly, olive-gray back, white eye ring, and a faint wing bar.  

---

## Habitat  
Inhabits coniferous and mixed forests, especially near streams, wetlands, or open areas with low vegetation.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across North America from Alaska to Newfoundland. Winters in Central America and northern South America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling from North America to Central and South America via the Caribbean and Gulf Coast.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily insects, including flies, beetles, and moths. Occasionally consumes berries in winter.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active during the day; territorial during breeding. Known for hovering briefly while hunting prey mid-air.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of sharp, high-pitched notes ("pip-pip-pip" or "tsee-tsee-tsee"). Call: A sharp "tik."  

---

## Breeding  
Builds a cup-shaped nest in coniferous trees or shrubs. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, shared by both parents. Fledging period: 12–14 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to logging and deforestation in breeding and wintering grounds.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and serves as an indicator of forest health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Often mistaken for similar Empidonax flycatchers due to subtle plumage differences.  
- Hovering behavior distinguishes it from many other flycatcher species.  
- Undertakes extensive migrations, traveling thousands of miles annually.
