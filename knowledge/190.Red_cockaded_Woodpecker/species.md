# Red-cockaded Woodpecker

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Leuconotopicus borealis  
Family: Picidae  
Order: Piciformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 20–24 cm  
Average Wingspan: 33–38 cm  
Average Weight: 35–50 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a black crown patch; females lack this and have a red crown with a black stripe.  
Distinctive Features: Red crown (males), white cheek stripe, and a black stripe through the eye.  

---

## Habitat  
Open pine forests, particularly longleaf pine ecosystems, with a mix of hardwoods. Requires mature trees for nesting.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Southeastern United States, including Georgia, South Carolina, Florida, Alabama, and parts of Texas.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Non-migratory  
Migration Pattern: N/A  

---

## Diet  
Insects, primarily ants, beetles, and other arthropods found in tree bark.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social, often seen in family groups. Excavates cavities in living pine trees, using resin to deter predators. Forms cooperative breeding groups with helpers.  

---

## Vocalization  
Calls include a sharp "kik" and a rolling "krrr." Drumming is used for communication and territory defense.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in cavities excavated in living pine trees. Clutch size: 2–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, shared by both parents. Fledging occurs at 28–30 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Endangered  
Threats: Habitat loss due to logging, urban development, and fire suppression.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations. Their nesting cavities provide shelter for other species, making them a keystone species.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Excavates nesting cavities in living trees, a unique behavior among woodpeckers.  
- Uses pine resin to coat cavities, deterring predators like snakes.  
- Forms cooperative breeding groups with non-breeding helpers assisting in raising young.
