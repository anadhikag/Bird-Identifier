# Greater Roadrunner

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Geococcyx californianus  
Family: Cuculidae  
Order: Cuculiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 43–50 cm  
Average Wingspan: 50–60 cm  
Average Weight: 200–300 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are slightly larger and have a more pronounced black-and-white plumage pattern.  
Distinctive Features: Long tail, short wings, and a distinctive black-and-white barred pattern on the back and wings.  

---

## Habitat  
Arid and semi-arid environments, including deserts, scrublands, and open woodlands. Prefers areas with low vegetation and sparse tree cover.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Native to the southwestern United States (Arizona, New Mexico, Texas) and northern Mexico.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Non-migratory  
Migration Pattern: Sedentary; remains in its range year-round.  

---

## Diet  
Insects, small reptiles, rodents, fruits, and cactus berries. Omnivorous, with diet varying by season.  

---

## Behaviour  
Terrestrial and fast runner, capable of reaching speeds up to 15 mph. Uses wings for balance while running. Active during the day.  

---

## Vocalization  
Distinctive call: A series of low, whistling, liquid notes. Also produces a sharp "krrr" sound when alarmed.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests are built in shrubs or cacti using sticks and twigs. Clutch size is 2–6 eggs, incubated by both parents for 18–20 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to urbanization, road collisions, and pesticide use.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect and rodent populations. Disperses seeds through fruit consumption.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Can run faster than it can fly.  
- Thermoregulates by spreading wings to absorb sunlight in cold weather.  
- Frequently depicted in pop culture, such as in the Looney Tunes character Wile E. Coyote.
