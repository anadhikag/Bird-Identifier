# White Pelican  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Pelecanus erythrorhynchos  
Family: Pelecanidae  
Order: Pelecaniformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 150 cm  
Average Wingspan: 244 cm  
Average Weight: 9 kg  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a longer, more robust bill than females.  
Distinctive Features: Large, pinkish bill with a distinctive throat pouch; white plumage; black wingtips visible in flight.  

---  

## Habitat  
White Pelicans inhabit shallow lakes, rivers, and coastal areas with abundant fish populations. They prefer freshwater environments but also occur in brackish or saltwater habitats.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Native to North and South America, ranging from the Great Lakes and the Gulf Coast in the U.S. to northern Argentina. Populations are concentrated in the interior of the U.S. and along the coasts of the Gulf of Mexico and Caribbean.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Breeds in North America and winters in the southern U.S., Mexico, and northern South America.  

---  

## Diet  
Primarily fish-eating (piscivorous), with a diet consisting of catfish, carp, and other freshwater or coastal fish species. Uses cooperative fishing techniques, herding fish into shallow water.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Social and gregarious, often seen in large groups. Uses its expandable throat pouch to scoop fish from water. Non-territorial during breeding but may compete for nesting sites.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Produces deep, guttural croaks and grunts, especially during social interactions or courtship displays.  

---  

## Breeding  
Nests in colonies on islands or in dense vegetation near water. Builds stick nests, laying 2–4 eggs per clutch. Both parents incubate eggs for 28–35 days. Chicks fledge at 6–8 weeks.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to wetland drainage, pollution, and human disturbance.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates fish populations in aquatic ecosystems. Serves as an indicator species for wetland health due to its dependence on clean, fish-rich habitats.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- Uses its bill pouch to carry up to 3 gallons of water while fishing.  
- Flies in tight, coordinated flocks during migration.  
- The oldest recorded White Pelican lived over 30 years in the wild.
