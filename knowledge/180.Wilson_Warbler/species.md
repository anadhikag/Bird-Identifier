# Wilson Warbler

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Cardellina pusilla  
Family: Parulidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 10–12 cm  
Average Wingspan: 16–18 cm  
Average Weight: 8–12 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a distinct black mask across the eyes; females have a duller mask or none.  
Distinctive Features: Bright yellow body, black mask in males, and two white wing bars.  

---

## Habitat  
Dense undergrowth in coniferous or mixed forests, especially near streams or wetlands.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in western North America, from Alaska to the northern United States. Winters in Central America and northern South America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migration from North American breeding grounds to Central and South American wintering grounds.  

---

## Diet  
Insects and spiders, primarily foraged on the ground or in low vegetation.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active and skulking, often flitting through underbrush. May follow mixed-species flocks during migration.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song consists of high-pitched, buzzy trills or a rapid series of notes resembling "tsee-tsee-tsee."  

---

## Breeding  
Nests are built on the ground or in low vegetation. Clutch size is 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12 days, primarily by the female. Both parents feed the young.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss in breeding and wintering grounds; pesticide use reducing insect availability.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and serves as an indicator of forest health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Named after Alexander Wilson, the father of American ornithology.  
- Follows army ants to catch insects flushed out by the ants.  
- One of the most common warblers in its range.
