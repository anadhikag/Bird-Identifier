# Pied Kingfisher

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Ceryle rudis  
Family: Cerylidae  
Order: Coraciiformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 23–28 cm  
Average Wingspan: 40–45 cm  
Average Weight: 100–140 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Slightly smaller females; otherwise similar plumage.  
Distinctive Features: Black and white plumage with a bold white collar, long tail, and habit of hovering before diving for prey.  

---

## Habitat  
Freshwater rivers, lakes, wetlands, and coastal areas with abundant perches and fish populations.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Widespread across sub-Saharan Africa, the Indian subcontinent, Southeast Asia, and parts of Australia.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Mostly resident; some populations migrate locally or seasonally in response to rainfall.  
Migration Pattern: Unknown for most populations.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily fish, but also eats insects, amphibians, and small crustaceans. Hunts by hovering and diving.  

---

## Behaviour  
Hovering while searching for prey is a defining behavior. Often seen singly or in pairs, perching on branches or wires near water.  

---

## Vocalization  
Loud, ringing calls including "kyeep" and "kyeep-kyeep," often repeated in series.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in hollows of trees, riverbanks, or termite mounds. Clutch size: 4–6 eggs. Incubation lasts 28–30 days, shared by both parents.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat degradation, pollution, and wetland loss.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates fish and insect populations. Serves as an indicator species for healthy aquatic ecosystems.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Hovering while hunting is unique among kingfishers.  
- Found on three continents: Africa, Asia, and Australia.  
- Nests in tree hollows or riverbank burrows, sometimes usurping other birds' nests.
