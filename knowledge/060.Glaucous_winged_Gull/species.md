# Glaucous-winged Gull

## Basic Information
Scientific Name:  
Larus glaucescens  
Family:  
Laridae  
Order:  
Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length:  
56–66 cm  
Average Wingspan:  
137–155 cm  
Average Weight:  
1.2–1.7 kg  
Male vs Female Differences:  
Sexes are similar in size and plumage; males may be slightly larger.  
Distinctive Features:  
Pale gray body, white head, dark wingtips, and a robust, hooked bill.  

---

## Habitat  
Coastal rocky shores, estuaries, islands, and urban areas near the ocean.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Pacific coast of North America, from Alaska to northern Mexico, with year-round populations in warmer regions.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status:  
Partially migratory.  
Migration Pattern:  
Northern populations migrate southward in winter; some remain in milder climates.  

---

## Diet  
Omnivorous, feeding on fish, crustaceans, mollusks, scavenged food, and human refuse.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and opportunistic; often seen in groups. Aggressive during nesting and food competition.  

---

## Vocalization  
Loud, harsh honking calls, often used during territorial disputes or courtship.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in colonies on cliffs or rocky outcrops. Clutch size: 2–3 eggs. Incubation lasts 28–32 days, shared by both parents.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status:  
Least Concern  
Threats:  
Habitat degradation, human disturbance, and pollution.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates invertebrate populations and scavenges carrion, aiding nutrient cycling.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Hybridizes with the Western Gull (*Larus occidentalis*) in some regions.  
- Known to use tools, such as dropping shells onto rocks to crack them open.  
- Lifespan in the wild can exceed 25 years.
