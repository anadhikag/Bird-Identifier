# Green-tailed Towhee

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Melospiza georgiana
Family: Passerellidae
Order: Passeriformes

---

## Identification
Average Length: 16–18 cm
Average Wingspan: 25–28 cm
Average Weight: 25–35 g
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a more vibrant greenish tail and rufous sides; females are duller with less pronounced coloration.
Distinctive Features: Greenish tail, white belly, and rufous flanks; males display a black head and back in breeding plumage.

---

## Habitat
Prefers dense underbrush in chaparral, thickets, forest edges, and shrublands. Requires cover for foraging and nesting.

---

## Geographic Distribution
Found across western North America, from southern Canada (British Columbia, Alberta) to northern Mexico. Year-round residents in parts of the southwestern U.S., with northern populations migrating south in winter.

---

## Migration
Migratory Status: Partially migratory
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate to the southwestern U.S. and northern Mexico; southern populations are resident.

---

## Diet
Insects, seeds, berries, and other plant matter. Forages on the ground by scratching leaf litter.

---

## Behaviour
Shy and elusive, often remaining hidden in dense vegetation. Territorial during breeding season; performs "walk, stop, hop" foraging movements.

---

## Vocalization
Song: A series of clear, whistled notes, often described as "drink-your-tea." Call: A sharp "chink" or "chit" sound.

---

## Breeding
Nests on the ground or in low shrubs. Clutch size: 3–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, primarily by the female. Both parents feed nestlings.

---

## Conservation
IUCN Status: Least Concern
Threats: Habitat loss due to land conversion, urbanization, and fire suppression reducing shrubland habitats.

---

## Ecological Importance
Seeds and insects in their diet contribute to nutrient cycling and pest control. Their ground-nesting habits influence soil structure.

---

## Interesting Facts
- The greenish tail is the most distinguishing feature from the Eastern Towhee.
- Forages primarily on the ground, using its feet to scratch through leaf litter.
- Some populations are year-round residents, while others migrate seasonally.
