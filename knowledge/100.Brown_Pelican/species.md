# Brown Pelican

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Pelecanus occidentalis  
Family: Pelecanidae  
Order: Pelecaniformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 90–110 cm  
Average Wingspan: 160–180 cm  
Average Weight: 2.7–5 kg  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a slightly larger bill.  
Distinctive Features: Large, curved bill with a throat pouch; brown plumage; white neck and breast; black wingtips.  

---

## Habitat  
Coastal regions, including tropical and subtropical islands, estuaries, and lagoons.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Found along the coasts of the Americas, from the southeastern United States to northern Argentina.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory.  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate southward in winter; tropical populations are resident.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily fish, including menhaden, mullet, and sardines. Uses plunge-diving to catch prey.  

---

## Behaviour  
Flies in groups, often in V-formations. Uses cooperative feeding tactics to herd fish.  

---

## Vocalization  
Distinctive braying or grunting calls, often heard during breeding displays.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in colonies on islands or coastal cliffs. Lays 2–4 eggs per clutch. Both parents incubate eggs and feed chicks.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pollution, and human disturbance.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates fish populations and serves as an indicator species for marine ecosystem health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Can dive from heights of 10–20 feet to catch fish.  
- The throat pouch can hold up to 3 gallons of water.  
- Declined due to DDT contamination in the 20th century but recovered after its ban.
