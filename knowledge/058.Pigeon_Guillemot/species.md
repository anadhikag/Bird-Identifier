# Pigeon Guillemot  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Cepphus gullius  
Family: Alcidae  
Order: Charadriiformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 28–30 cm  
Average Wingspan: 50–55 cm  
Average Weight: 200–250 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males slightly larger with longer tail; otherwise similar plumage.  
Distinctive Features: Black upperparts, white underparts, black breast band, white wing stripe, red eye, and short, thick bill.  

---  

## Habitat  
Coastal rocky shores, islands, and tidal pools. Nests in crevices, under rocks, or in burrows near water.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Pacific coast of North America, from Alaska to Baja California, with populations in the Bering Sea and Aleutian Islands.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory.  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate south in winter; some remain year-round in milder climates.  

---  

## Diet  
Fish (e.g., herring, smelt), crustaceans, and mollusks. Dives from the surface to catch prey.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Social and gregarious; often seen in small groups. Skilled diver, using wings to "fly" underwater. Active during the day.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Loud, whistling calls, often described as "kyow-kyow" or "whoo-whoo," used in courtship and territorial displays.  

---  

## Breeding  
Nests in rocky crevices or under boulders. Clutch size: 2–3 eggs. Incubation lasts 28–30 days, shared by both parents. Chicks fledge at 28–35 days.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, oil spills, pollution, and human disturbance.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Controls populations of fish and invertebrates. Indicator species for coastal ecosystem health.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- Can "fly" underwater using wing propulsion to chase prey.  
- Red eyes are a key field mark for identification.  
- Nests in rocky crevices to avoid predators like gulls and foxes.
