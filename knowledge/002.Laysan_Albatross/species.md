# Laysan Albatross  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Phoebastria immutabilis  
Family: Diomedeidae  
Order: Procellariiformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 80–90 cm  
Average Wingspan: 200–230 cm  
Average Weight: 3–4 kg  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are slightly larger and have a more robust bill.  
Distinctive Features: White body with dark gray wings, a yellow bill, and a distinctive black mask around the eyes.  

---  

## Habitat  
Breeds on low-lying tropical islands; spends most of its life at sea, particularly in the North Pacific Ocean.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Primarily found in the North Pacific Ocean, with breeding colonies on the Hawaiian Islands, Midway Atoll, and the Bonin Islands.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partial migrant  
Migration Pattern: Non-breeding adults disperse widely across the North Pacific, while juveniles may wander further south.  

---  

## Diet  
Primarily feeds on squid and small fish, which it captures by surface-seizing or shallow diving. Also scavenges on floating debris.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Highly social at sea and during breeding season. Uses dynamic soaring to glide efficiently over open water. Performs elaborate courtship displays with synchronized movements.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Emits soft, grunting calls during the breeding season. Noisy colonies produce a chorus of vocalizations during nesting.  

---  

## Breeding  
Monogamous pairs nest on the ground in colonies. Lays a single egg per clutch. Both parents share incubation duties, which last approximately 65 days. Chicks fledge after 100–120 days.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Plastic pollution, longline fishing bycatch, and habitat loss from rising sea levels and invasive species on breeding islands.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Acts as an indicator species for ocean health. Helps control squid and fish populations. Carcasses and guano contribute to nutrient cycling in island ecosystems.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- Laysan Albatrosses can live over 50 years and often mate for life.  
- They use dynamic soaring to travel thousands of kilometers without flapping their wings.  
- A single egg is laid annually, and both parents take turns guarding it for months.
