# Black-billed Cuckoo

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Coccyzus erythrophthalmus  
Family: Cuculidae  
Order: Cuculiformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 28–33 cm  
Average Wingspan: 46–51 cm  
Average Weight: 45–75 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Slight size difference; males slightly larger  
Distinctive Features: Black bill, long tail with white outer feathers, barred underparts, and striking red eyes  

---

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, forest edges, shrubby thickets, and riparian areas with scattered trees. Prefers habitats with a mix of vegetation for foraging and nesting.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across eastern North America, from southern Canada to the northern United States. Winters in northern South America, including Colombia, Venezuela, and the Guianas.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling from North America to northern South America. Arrives in breeding range in spring; departs in late summer.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily insects, including caterpillars, beetles, grasshoppers, and cicadas. Also consumes fruits and berries during winter months.  

---

## Behaviour  
Solitary and elusive, often foraging alone in trees or shrubs. Known to eat hairy caterpillars avoided by other birds. Shy and difficult to observe due to cryptic plumage.  

---

## Vocalization  
Distinctive vocalizations include a rapid, repetitive "ko-ko-ko" call and a whistled "cu-cu" during the breeding season. Calls are often heard before the bird is seen.  

---

## Breeding  
Builds flimsy, open-cup nests in trees or shrubs. Lays 2–4 pale blue or greenish eggs. Incubation lasts 10–12 days, shared by both parents. Chicks fledge in 8–10 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pesticide use, and climate change affecting insect populations.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations, particularly defoliating caterpillars, which can reduce forest damage. Acts as an indicator species for ecosystem health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Specializes in eating hairy caterpillars that other birds avoid.  
- Known as the "rain-crow" for its association with stormy weather.  
- Has a unique ability to digest toxic insects that deter predators.
