# Spotted Catbird  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Ailuroedus melanotis  
Family: Cuculidae  
Order: Cuculiformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 35–40 cm  
Average Wingspan: 45–50 cm  
Average Weight: 100–150 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are slightly larger and have more vibrant plumage; females are duller.  
Distinctive Features: Black and white spotted plumage, black facial mask, long tail, and a loud, whistling call.  

---  

## Habitat  
Tropical rainforests, particularly in dense undergrowth and along forest edges in the Wet Tropics of Queensland, Australia.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Endemic to northeastern Australia, primarily in Queensland, with populations in the Atherton Tablelands and surrounding regions.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Non-migratory  
Migration Pattern: Resident within its range year-round.  

---  

## Diet  
Insectivorous, feeding on insects, spiders, and other invertebrates. Occasionally consumes small fruits.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Shy and elusive, often foraging on the forest floor. Solitary or in pairs. Known for its loud, whistling calls despite its secretive nature.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Produces a series of clear, fluting whistles, often described as a repetitive "kyow-kyow-kyow" pattern. Calls are used for communication and territorial defense.  

---  

## Breeding  
Breeds in the warmer months (October–March). Nests are built in trees using twigs and leaves. Clutch size is 2–3 eggs, with incubation shared by both parents.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to deforestation, land clearing, and climate change impacts on rainforest ecosystems.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Plays a role in controlling insect populations and seed dispersal through occasional fruit consumption. Indicator species for rainforest health.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- The spotted plumage provides camouflage in dappled forest light.  
- Known for its loud, distinctive call despite being a shy, ground-foraging bird.  
- Endemic to Australia, found only in the Wet Tropics region.
