# Orange-crowned Warbler

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Oreothlypis celata  
Family: Parulidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 11–13 cm  
Average Wingspan: 18–21 cm  
Average Weight: 9–14 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a brighter yellow crown stripe; females are duller with a less distinct crown.  
Distinctive Features: Yellow crown (more pronounced in males), olive-green back, white underparts with faint eye ring, and short, rounded wings.  

---

## Habitat  
Dense, low shrubs in chaparral, thickets, coniferous forests, and riparian areas. Prefers brushy habitats with tangled vegetation.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across western North America from Alaska and Canada to northern Mexico. Winters in the southern U.S., Mexico, and Central America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Migrates southward to wintering grounds in the southern U.S., Mexico, and Central America; some populations remain in milder climates year-round.  

---

## Diet  
Insects and spiders, including beetles, flies, caterpillars, and ants. Occasionally consumes berries and seeds.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active and restless, foraging by flitting through foliage. Often seen in dense vegetation, where it gleans insects from leaves and branches.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A high-pitched, variable trill or series of rapid notes. Call: A sharp, metallic "tik" or "chink."  

---

## Breeding  
Breeds in spring and summer. Builds a cup-shaped nest of twigs, grass, and moss in shrubs or low trees. Clutch size: 3–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to urbanization, agriculture, and wildfires.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and serves as prey for larger birds and mammals.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Often difficult to observe due to its preference for dense, thorny shrubs.  
- Hybridizes with Yellow Warbler in some regions where their ranges overlap.  
- Known for its subtle plumage changes between breeding and non-breeding seasons.
