# Lazuli Bunting

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Passerina amoena  
Family: Cardinalidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 13–14 cm  
Average Wingspan: 19–21 cm  
Average Weight: 14–18 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have bright blue heads, rose-red chest, and grayish-blue underparts; females are duller with brown and gray plumage, streaked underparts.  
Distinctive Features: Males exhibit a striking blue head and rose-red throat, while females have a pale eyebrow stripe and streaked breast.  

---

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, sagebrush, riparian thickets, and agricultural areas with scattered shrubs.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in western North America from southern Canada to northern Mexico; winters in the southwestern U.S., Mexico, and Central America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Migrates southward from northern breeding grounds to wintering areas in the U.S. Southwest, Mexico, and Central America.  

---

## Diet  
Seeds, insects (especially beetles and grasshoppers), and berries.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social in non-breeding seasons, often foraging in flocks. Males perform flight displays during courtship.  

---

## Vocalization  
A series of short, musical notes: "see-see-see" or "weet-weet-weet," often ending with a rising trill.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in low shrubs or trees; females build cup-shaped nests. Clutch size: 2–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, primarily by the female.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to agricultural expansion and urban development.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Seeds dispersers and insect controllers in their habitats.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- The male’s blue plumage derives its name from the color of lapis lazuli gemstones.  
- Males sing from elevated perches to defend territories and attract mates.  
- Hybridization with Indigo Buntings occurs where their ranges overlap.
