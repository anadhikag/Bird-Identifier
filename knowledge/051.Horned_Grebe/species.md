# Horned Grebe

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Podiceps auritus  
Family: Podicipedidae  
Order: Podicipediformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 30–40 cm  
Average Wingspan: 55–65 cm  
Average Weight: 300–700 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males slightly larger; both sexes similar in plumage.  
Distinctive Features: Black and white breeding plumage, red eyes, and feathered "horns" behind the eyes. Non-breeding adults have grayish-brown plumage.  

---

## Habitat  
Freshwater lakes, ponds, and marshes during breeding season; coastal bays and estuaries in winter.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across North America and Europe; winters in the southern United States, Mexico, and along the coasts of Europe and Asia.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migration from northern breeding grounds to temperate and subtropical wintering areas.  

---

## Diet  
Aquatic invertebrates (e.g., insects, crustaceans), small fish, and aquatic plants.  

---

## Behaviour  
Solitary or in small groups; highly skilled diver, using both feet for propulsion. Performs synchronized courtship displays with head movements and swimming.  

---

## Vocalization  
Calls include grunts, croaks, and soft whistles. Vocalizations increase during courtship and territorial disputes.  

---

## Breeding  
Builds floating nests of aquatic vegetation anchored to plants. Lays 2–5 eggs; both parents incubate for 21–25 days. Chicks are precocial and capable of swimming shortly after hatching.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to wetland drainage, pollution, and climate change impacts on water levels.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates populations of aquatic invertebrates and small fish. Serves as an indicator species for wetland health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Dives up to 20 meters to forage, using both feet to propel itself underwater.  
- Performs elaborate courtship rituals involving synchronized swimming and head movements.  
- Non-breeding adults adopt a cryptic, gray-brown plumage to blend into their surroundings.
