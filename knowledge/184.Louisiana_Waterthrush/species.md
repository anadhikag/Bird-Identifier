# Louisiana Waterthrush

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Parkesia motacilla  
Family: Parulidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 14–16 cm  
Average Wingspan: 20–22 cm  
Average Weight: 18–23 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males and females are similar in appearance, though males may be slightly larger.  
Distinctive Features: White eye ring, yellow eyebrow stripe (supercilium), and long tail with white outer feathers.  

---

## Habitat  
Forested wetlands, especially near clear, fast-flowing streams and rivers with abundant leaf litter. Prefers areas with dense understory vegetation.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in eastern North America, from the Great Lakes region to the Gulf Coast. Winters in Central America, from southern Mexico to northern Colombia.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migration; travels from breeding grounds to Central America, often using both coastal and inland routes.  

---

## Diet  
Insects, spiders, and aquatic invertebrates. Forages on the ground, picking prey from leaf litter near water.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active foragers, often seen bobbing their tails while walking. Territorial during breeding season; sings from perches near water.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of clear, whistled notes, often described as "fee-bee-bee-bee" or "fee-bee-bee-bee-bee." Call: Sharp "tik" or "chink."  

---

## Breeding  
Nests on the ground near streams, using leaves, twigs, and moss. Clutch size: 3–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, primarily by the female. Both parents feed nestlings.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss from deforestation, water pollution, and stream degradation.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Indicates ecosystem health due to sensitivity to water quality. Controls insect populations and contributes to nutrient cycling in wetlands.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Uses tail-flicking behavior to stir leaf litter and expose prey.  
- Prefers unpolluted streams, making it a bioindicator species.  
- Nests are often built in abandoned nests of other birds, such as thrushes.
