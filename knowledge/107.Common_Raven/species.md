# Common Raven

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Corvus corax  
Family: Corvidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 60–70 cm  
Average Wingspan: 120–150 cm  
Average Weight: 600–1500 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Slight size variation; females may have smaller bills.  
Distinctive Features: All-black plumage, heavy bill, wedge-shaped tail, and glossy feathers with iridescent sheen.  

---

## Habitat  
Common Ravens inhabit diverse environments, including mountains, forests, deserts, tundras, and urban areas. They prefer open spaces with scattered trees or cliffs for nesting.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Widely distributed across the Northern Hemisphere, from North America (Alaska to Mexico) and Eurasia (Scandinavia to Japan) to the Middle East and North Africa.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate south in winter; tropical and temperate populations are sedentary.  

---

## Diet  
Omnivorous, feeding on carrion, insects, small mammals, fruits, seeds, and human refuse. Known for scavenging and opportunistic foraging.  

---

## Behaviour  
Highly intelligent, social, and curious. Exhibit problem-solving skills, tool use, and complex communication. Often travel in pairs or small groups.  

---

## Vocalization  
Produce deep, resonant croaks, guttural grunts, and mimic sounds. Vocalizations vary by region and include alarm calls, courtship songs, and mimicry of other species.  

---

## Breeding  
Monogamous pairs build large stick nests in trees, cliffs, or human structures. Clutch size: 3–7 eggs. Incubation lasts 18–22 days, primarily by the female. Both parents feed nestlings.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, human persecution, and poisoning; populations remain stable due to adaptability.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Scavengers that reduce disease spread by consuming carrion. Seed dispersers and predators of insects and small animals, contributing to ecosystem balance.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Use tools to extract food from crevices.  
- Recognized for complex social bonds and cooperative problem-solving.  
- Lifespan in the wild can exceed 20 years.
