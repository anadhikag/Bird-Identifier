# Blue Grosbeak

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Cyanocompsa cyanoides
Family: Cardinalidae
Order: Passeriformes

---

## Identification
Average Length: 18–21 cm
Average Wingspan: 25–30 cm
Average Weight: 28–40 g
Male vs Female Differences: Males are predominantly blue with chestnut wing patches and a black mask; females are more subdued with brownish tones and lack the blue plumage.
Distinctive Features: Bright blue plumage in males, conical beak adapted for seed and insect consumption, and chestnut wing patches.

---

## Habitat
Blue Grosbeaks inhabit open woodlands, thickets, and shrubby areas, especially near water sources. They are commonly found in riparian zones and agricultural fields with scattered trees.

---

## Geographic Distribution
Blue Grosbeaks breed in the southern United States and northern Mexico, ranging from Texas and Oklahoma to Arizona and New Mexico. They winter in Central America and northern South America.

---

## Migration
Migratory Status: Migratory
Migration Pattern: Blue Grosbeaks migrate from their breeding grounds in North America to their wintering grounds in Central and South America.

---

## Diet
Their diet consists of seeds, fruits, and insects. Insects are particularly important during the breeding season for protein.

---

## Behaviour
Blue Grosbeaks are generally solitary or found in pairs. They are territorial during the breeding season and may be seen foraging on the ground or in low vegetation.

---

## Vocalization
Their song is a series of musical, whistled notes, often described as soft and varied. They also produce sharp calls when alarmed.

---

## Breeding
Blue Grosbeaks build cup-shaped nests in dense shrubs or low trees. The female lays 2–4 eggs, which are incubated for about 12–14 days. Both parents feed the young.

---

## Conservation
IUCN Status: Least Concern
Threats: Habitat loss due to deforestation and agricultural expansion poses a threat to their populations.

---

## Ecological Importance
Blue Grosbeaks contribute to seed dispersal and insect control in their habitats. Their presence is an indicator of healthy shrubland ecosystems.

---

## Interesting Facts
- Blue Grosbeaks are known for their striking blue plumage in males, which is used to attract mates.
- They are often found near water sources, such as rivers and streams.
- These birds are sometimes mistaken for indigo buntings due to their similar coloration.
