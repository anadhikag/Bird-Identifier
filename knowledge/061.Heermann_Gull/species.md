# Heermann's Gull

## Basic Information
Scientific Name:  
Larus heermanni  
Family:  
Laridae  
Order:  
Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length:  
46–51 cm  
Average Wingspan:  
122–135 cm  
Average Weight:  
700–900 g  
Male vs Female Differences:  
Unknown  
Distinctive Features:  
Dark mantle, pale head, yellow bill with black spot, dark wingtips with white trailing edges.

---

## Habitat  
Coastal areas, rocky shores, islands, and estuaries.

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Primarily found along the Pacific coast of Baja California, Mexico.

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status:  
Resident  
Migration Pattern:  
Non-migratory; remains in its coastal range year-round.

---

## Diet  
Fish, invertebrates, carrion, and human refuse.

---

## Behaviour  
Typically found in small groups; forages along shorelines and in shallow waters.

---

## Vocalization  
Harsh, barking calls similar to other gull species.

---

## Breeding  
Nests on rocky cliffs or islands, laying 1–3 eggs per clutch. Incubation is shared by both parents.

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status:  
Least Concern  
Threats:  
Habitat degradation, pollution, and human disturbance.

---

## Ecological Importance  
Plays a role in coastal ecosystems by scavenging and controlling populations of invertebrates.

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Named after the German naturalist Hermann von Meyer.  
- Often mistaken for the California Gull due to similar plumage.  
- Known for its opportunistic feeding habits and adaptability to human-modified environments.
