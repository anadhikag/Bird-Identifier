# White-necked Raven

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Corvus crassirostris
Family: Corvidae
Order: Passeriformes

---

## Identification
Average Length: 55–65 cm
Average Wingspan: 110–120 cm
Average Weight: 500–700 g
Male vs Female Differences: Sexes are similar in appearance; females may be slightly smaller.
Distinctive Features: Black body with a white neck collar, strong bill, and long tail.

---

## Habitat
White-necked Ravens inhabit a variety of open and semi-open habitats, including savannas, grasslands, agricultural areas, and forest edges. They are often found near human settlements.

---

## Geographic Distribution
This species is native to parts of Africa, with a range that includes countries such as Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, and parts of southern Africa.

---

## Migration
Migratory Status: Non-migratory
Migration Pattern: Unknown

---

## Diet
White-necked Ravens are omnivorous and opportunistic feeders. Their diet includes insects, small vertebrates, fruits, seeds, and human refuse.

---

## Behaviour
These birds are highly intelligent and social, often seen in pairs or small groups. They are known for their curiosity and adaptability, especially in human-modified environments.

---

## Vocalization
The vocalizations of the White-necked Raven include a variety of calls, such as croaks, grunts, and harsh cries. These calls are used for communication between individuals and during social interactions.

---

## Breeding
Breeding occurs during the rainy season. Nests are built in trees or on cliffs, and the clutch size is typically two to three eggs. Incubation and chick-rearing duties are shared between the sexes.

---

## Conservation
IUCN Status: Least Concern
Threats: Habitat loss and human persecution are potential threats to local populations.

---

## Ecological Importance
White-necked Ravens play a role in controlling insect populations and seed dispersal. They also contribute to nutrient cycling through scavenging activities.

---

## Interesting Facts
- The White-necked Raven is closely related to the Common Raven but is distinguished by its white neck collar.
- These birds are known to use tools in some populations, showcasing their problem-solving abilities.
- They are often seen near roadsides, where they scavenge for food.
