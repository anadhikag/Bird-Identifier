# Rose-breasted Grosbeak

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Pheucticus ludovicianus  
Family: Cardinalidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 19–22 cm  
Average Wingspan: 25–28 cm  
Average Weight: 28–35 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are strikingly black and white with a rose-red breast patch; females are brown-streaked with a pale eyebrow and dark throat.  
Distinctive Features: Large, conical beak; male’s vivid coloration; female’s streaked plumage.  

---

## Habitat  
Deciduous forests, especially near water sources such as rivers and swamps.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across eastern Canada and the northern United States; winters in Central America and northern South America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling from North America to Central and northern South America.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily seeds, fruits, and insects. Forages on the ground and in trees.  

---

## Behaviour  
Solitary and territorial during breeding. Active during the day, often seen foraging or singing from treetops.  

---

## Vocalization  
Produces a rich, flute-like song with varied whistles and warbles.  

---

## Breeding  
Monogamous. Nests are cup-shaped, built in trees using twigs and leaves. Clutch size: 2–4 eggs. Incubation period: 12–13 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pesticide use, and climate change.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and aids in seed dispersal through fruit consumption.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Males perform courtship displays involving wing-fluttering and song.  
- Known for mimicking other birds’ calls during vocalizations.  
- Frequently visits backyard feeders for sunflower seeds.
