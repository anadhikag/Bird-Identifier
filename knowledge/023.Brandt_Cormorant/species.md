# Brandt's Cormorant

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Phalacrocorax penicillatus  
Family: Phalacrocoracidae  
Order: Suliformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 75–85 cm  
Average Wingspan: 115–130 cm  
Average Weight: 1.8–3.2 kg  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are slightly larger and have a more pronounced white throat patch.  
Distinctive Features: Black plumage with a white throat patch, long hooked bill, and a distinctive "hooded" appearance during breeding season.  

---

## Habitat  
Coastal rocky shores, estuaries, and marine environments. Prefers areas with abundant fish populations.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Pacific coast of North America, from Alaska to Baja California, with year-round presence in California.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Non-migratory in southern populations; northern birds may move south in winter.  
Migration Pattern: Short-distance movements along the Pacific coast.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily fish (e.g., herring, anchovies, sardines) and cephalopods (e.g., squid).  

---

## Behaviour  
Dives from the surface to catch prey, often swimming underwater. Frequently seen fishing in groups. Perches on rocks to dry wings after diving.  

---

## Vocalization  
Gruff, croaking calls used during courtship and territorial disputes.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in colonies on coastal cliffs or islands. Both parents incubate 3–4 eggs for 28–31 days. Chicks fledge at 6–8 weeks.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Oil spills, overfishing, and habitat degradation from coastal development.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates fish populations and serves as an indicator species for marine ecosystem health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Uses underwater swimming to pursue prey, reaching depths of up to 15 meters.  
- Nesting colonies can number in the thousands, with synchronized courtship displays.  
- Known to "fish cooperatively" by herding schools of fish toward the surface.
