# Northern Mockingbird  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Mimus polyglottos  
Family: Mimidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 9–11 in (23–28 cm)  
Average Wingspan: 12–14 in (30–35 cm)  
Average Weight: 1.4–2.0 oz (40–57 g)  
Male vs Female Differences: Sexes are similar in appearance; males may appear slightly larger.  
Distinctive Features: Gray and white plumage with black eye stripe, long tail, and ability to mimic other birds' songs.  

---  

## Habitat  
Open habitats with scattered trees, including suburban areas, parks, gardens, and scrublands.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Native to North America, ranging from southern Canada through the United States and into Mexico.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partial migrant  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate southward in winter; tropical populations are resident.  

---  

## Diet  
Insectivorous, feeding on insects, spiders, and arthropods; also consumes fruits and berries seasonally.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Territorial and aggressive, especially during breeding season. Known for complex songs and mimicry of other birds and mechanical sounds.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Produces a long, varied song with repeated phrases and mimicked sounds. Calls include sharp, whistled notes and harsh alarm calls.  

---  

## Breeding  
Nests in trees or shrubs; clutch size is 2–5 eggs. Both parents incubate eggs and care for young. Breeding season spans spring to early fall.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pesticide use, and collisions with vehicles.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and disperses seeds through fruit consumption.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- Mimics over 200 different bird species and mechanical sounds.  
- Defends territory aggressively, attacking predators like cats or hawks.  
- Known for singing at dawn and dusk, often continuing into night during breeding season.
