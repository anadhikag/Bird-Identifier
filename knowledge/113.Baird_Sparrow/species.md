# Baird's Sparrow

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Ammodramus bairdii  
Family: Passerellidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 12–14 cm  
Average Wingspan: 18–20 cm  
Average Weight: 14–18 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have more distinct black and white head markings; females are slightly duller.  
Distinctive Features: Black and white head pattern, buffy sides, dark eye line, and a short, rounded tail.  

---

## Habitat  
Native to grasslands, including tallgrass prairies, shrub-steppe, and sagebrush habitats. Prefers open areas with sparse vegetation for foraging.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in central North America, from southern Canada (Saskatchewan to Texas) through the U.S. Great Plains. Winters in the southern U.S., northern Mexico, and the Gulf Coast.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Travels from breeding grounds to wintering areas in the southern U.S. and northern Mexico; some populations are partially migratory.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily granivorous, feeding on seeds; also consumes insects and arthropods, especially during breeding season. Forages on the ground.  

---

## Behaviour  
Skulks low in dense vegetation, often remaining hidden. Shy and elusive, with rapid, low flights between cover. Social during migration and winter flocks.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of short, musical notes: *tsee-tsee-tsee-see*. Call: A sharp, high-pitched *tik*.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests on the ground in grassy or shrubby habitats. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 11–13 days, shared by both parents. Chicks fledge at 8–10 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to agricultural conversion, urbanization, and grassland fragmentation.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Seeds dispersal and insect population control. Serves as prey for raptors and small mammals. Indicator species for grassland health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- ...Nests are built in shallow depressions lined with fine grasses and feathers.  
- ...Prefers undisturbed grasslands, avoiding areas with heavy grazing or invasive species.  
- ...Males perform flight displays during courtship, singing while hovering above the ground.
