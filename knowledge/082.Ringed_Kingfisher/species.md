# Ringed Kingfisher

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Ceryle torquata  
Family: Cerylidae  
Order: Coraciiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 28–34 cm  
Average Wingspan: 40–46 cm  
Average Weight: 140–220 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a solid black bill, while females have a black and yellow bill.  
Distinctive Features: Blue and white plumage with a black neck ring, white underparts with black barring, and a long, sharp bill.  

---

## Habitat  
Inhabits rivers, streams, lakes, and coastal areas with overhanging branches for perching and diving. Requires clear water for fishing.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Found across Europe, Africa, and parts of Asia, including the Middle East and South Asia. Absent from the Americas.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate southward in winter; tropical populations are resident.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily fish, supplemented by amphibians, crustaceans, and large insects. Hunts by perching and plunging into water.  

---

## Behaviour  
Solitary and territorial. Often seen perched on branches or wires above water. Dives headfirst to catch prey. Reuses nest holes in riverbanks or trees.  

---

## Vocalization  
Loud, ringing calls including a sharp "kye-ee" and a series of metallic "kik-kik-kik" notes.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in burrows dug into riverbanks or in tree cavities. Clutch size: 4–6 eggs. Incubation lasts 19–21 days, shared by both parents.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat degradation, water pollution, and loss of riverine vegetation.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls aquatic prey populations and serves as an indicator of healthy freshwater ecosystems.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Can hover briefly before diving to pinpoint prey.  
- The black neck ring is more pronounced in males.  
- Reuses nest sites for multiple breeding seasons.
