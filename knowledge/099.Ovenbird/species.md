# Ovenbird

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Seiurus aurocapilla  
Family: Parulidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 14–16 cm  
Average Wingspan: 20–23 cm  
Average Weight: 18–25 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a more vibrant golden crown; females are duller.  
Distinctive Features: Brown upperparts, white underparts, black eye stripe, and a prominent golden "hood."  

---

## Habitat  
Dense deciduous or mixed forests with thick understories. Prefers mature woodlands and avoids open areas.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across eastern North America, from southern Canada to the Gulf Coast. Winters in Central America, including Mexico, Guatemala, and Honduras.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migrants traveling from North America to Central America.  

---

## Diet  
Insects and spiders, primarily foraged on the forest floor by flipping leaves with their feet.  

---

## Behaviour  
Solitary except during breeding. Builds a dome-shaped nest on the ground. Known for its loud, ringing song during the breeding season.  

---

## Vocalization  
A clear, whistled "teacher-teacher-teacher" call, often repeated and used for territory defense.  

---

## Breeding  
Constructs a cup-shaped nest on the forest floor, lined with moss and lichen. Clutch size: 2–4 eggs. Both parents incubate and feed the altricial young.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss in breeding and wintering grounds, collisions with buildings during migration.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and contributes to seed dispersal through foraging activities.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Builds a unique "oven-shaped" nest on the ground for camouflage.  
- Their song is often mistaken for a human voice saying "teacher."  
- They are one of the most common warblers in eastern North America.
