# White-breasted Kingfisher

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Halcyon smyrnensis  
Family: Alcedinidae  
Order: Coraciiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 25–28 cm  
Average Wingspan: 40–45 cm  
Average Weight: 100–150 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a slightly longer beak; otherwise, sexes are similar.  
Distinctive Features: Bright blue back, white underparts, black and white head pattern, and a long, graduated tail.  

---

## Habitat  
Inhabits wetlands, rivers, lakes, and open woodlands. Also found in agricultural areas and near human settlements.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Native to South and Southeast Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, Myanmar, Thailand, and parts of China.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate seasonally to southern regions during winter.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily fish, but also consumes insects, amphibians, and small crustaceans. Hunts by perching and diving.  

---

## Behaviour  
Territorial and solitary. Often seen perched on branches or wires near water. Known for bold, aggressive behavior.  

---

## Vocalization  
Emits a loud, ringing "kik-kik-kik" or "kek-kek-kek" call, often repeated in sequences.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in burrows dug into riverbanks or earthy slopes. Clutch size is 2–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 18–20 days, shared by both parents.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, wetland degradation, and human disturbance.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls populations of aquatic insects and small fish. Indicator species for wetland health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Can hover briefly while scanning for prey.  
- Uses a specialized beak to spear fish underwater.  
- Often seen near human settlements, adapting to urban environments.
