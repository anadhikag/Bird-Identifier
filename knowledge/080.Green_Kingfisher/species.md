# Green Kingfisher  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Chloroceryle americana  
Family: Alcedinidae  
Order: Coraciiformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 18–20 cm  
Average Wingspan: 28–31 cm  
Average Weight: 38–45 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a more pronounced green cap and slightly larger size; females may have a fainter black mask.  
Distinctive Features: Bright green plumage, black mask extending from eye to nape, long straight bill, and a white throat.  

---  

## Habitat  
Inhabits freshwater environments such as rivers, streams, lakes, and reservoirs with overhanging vegetation. Requires muddy or sandy banks for nesting.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Native to the Americas, ranging from southern Texas and Mexico through Central America to northern South America (Colombia and Venezuela).  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory.  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate to Central America during winter; tropical populations are resident.  

---  

## Diet  
Primarily fish, supplemented by amphibians, crustaceans, and large insects. Hunts by perching and diving headfirst into water.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Territorial and solitary. Uses its bill to probe or strike prey underwater. Often perches conspicuously on branches over water.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Loud, rattling, metallic "kek-kek-kek" calls, often repeated in sequences. Also produces harsh chattering during territorial disputes.  

---  

## Breeding  
Nests in burrows dug into riverbanks or sandbars. Clutch size: 4–7 eggs. Incubation lasts 18–20 days, shared by both parents.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat degradation from dam construction, pollution, and loss of riverine vegetation.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Acts as an indicator species for freshwater ecosystem health. Controls populations of small aquatic prey.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- Hover-hunts over water before diving to catch fish.  
- Nests in burrows up to 2 meters deep in riverbanks.  
- The oldest recorded Green Kingfisher lived 13 years in the wild.
