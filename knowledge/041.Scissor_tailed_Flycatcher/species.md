# Scissor-tailed Flycatcher

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Tyrannus forficatus  
Family: Tyrannidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 17–21 in (43–53 cm)  
Average Wingspan: 13–14 in (33–35 cm)  
Average Weight: 1.5–2 oz (43–57 g)  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have slightly longer tail streamers; otherwise, plumage is similar.  
Distinctive Features: Long, scissor-like tail feathers; black-and-white plumage; agile, acrobatic flight.  

---

## Habitat  
Open grasslands, agricultural fields, riparian areas, and suburban environments. Often perches on fence posts, utility wires, or dead trees.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in the southern United States (Texas to Florida) and northern Mexico. Winters in Central America and northern South America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling from North America to Central and South America for winter.  

---

## Diet  
Insects, including grasshoppers, beetles, flies, and dragonflies, caught in flight. Occasionally consumes small fruits.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and gregarious outside the breeding season. Performs aerial displays with tail-wagging and acrobatic maneuvers. Perches conspicuously and is territorial during nesting.  

---

## Vocalization  
Calls include a sharp "pip" or "chit," and a series of high-pitched, whistled notes during flight.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in trees, shrubs, or on man-made structures. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 14–15 days, shared by both parents. Young fledge in 14–18 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to agricultural expansion and urban development.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and serves as an indicator species for grassland ecosystem health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Possesses the longest tail relative to body size of any North American bird.  
- Performs tail-wagging displays during courtship and territorial disputes.  
- Capable of hovering briefly while catching insects mid-air.
