# Evening Grosbeak

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Coccothraustes vespertinus  
Family: Fringillidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 18–21 cm  
Average Wingspan: 25–28 cm  
Average Weight: 35–50 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have bright yellow underparts, a black hood, and white wing patches; females are more subdued with grayish heads and less vibrant underparts.  
Distinctive Features: Large, conical beak; males display iridescent black-and-yellow plumage; females have a pale eyebrow stripe.  

---

## Habitat  
Coniferous and mixed forests, especially near mountain ash trees. Also found in suburban areas with mature trees.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across Canada and the northern U.S. (Alaska to New England). Winters in the southern U.S., Mexico, and the Caribbean.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migrants; some populations move southward in winter, while others remain in northern regions if food is available.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily seeds (especially mountain ash and boxelder), fruits, and insects (in summer).  

---

## Behaviour  
Active and acrobatic, often hanging upside down to feed. Known to cache food in bark crevices. Social in winter flocks.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of whistled, musical notes. Call: A sharp, metallic "chink" or "tink."  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in coniferous trees; clutch size 2–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, primarily by the female.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, climate change impacts on food sources, and collisions with vehicles.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Seeds dispersers for mountain ash and other trees. Help control insect populations during breeding season.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Once common in urban areas due to their attraction to bird feeders.  
- Males' iridescent coloration results from feather structure, not pigmentation.  
- Capable of cracking open hard seeds using their powerful beaks.
