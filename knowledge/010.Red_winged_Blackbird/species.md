# Red-winged Blackbird

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Agelaius phoeniceus  
Family: Icteridae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 20–24 cm  
Average Wingspan: 31–37 cm  
Average Weight: 40–100 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have glossy black plumage with bright red and yellow shoulder patches; females are brown-streaked with a lighter head.  
Distinctive Features: Males display striking red epaulets; females resemble large sparrows with streaked underparts.  

---

## Habitat  
Wetlands, marshes, cattail stands, and agricultural fields. Prefers dense vegetation near water.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
North America, from southern Canada to northern South America. Breeds across the United States and southern Canada; winters in the southern U.S., Mexico, and Central America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partial migrant  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate south for winter; tropical populations are resident.  

---

## Diet  
Seeds, grains, and insects. Feeds on wetland plants, rice, and cultivated crops.  

---

## Behaviour  
Highly territorial during breeding season. Males perch prominently to sing and display red epaulets. Aggressive toward intruders.  

---

## Vocalization  
Loud, raspy "konk-la-ree" song and sharp, metallic calls. Males sing from elevated perches to defend territory.  

---

## Breeding  
Males defend territories and attract multiple females. Nests are built by females in dense vegetation. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 10–13 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Wetland loss, pesticide use, and agricultural expansion.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and disperses seeds. Indicator species for wetland health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Males defend territories aggressively, even attacking predators like snakes.  
- Females build nests in cattails or reeds, often near water.  
- Known for their role in studying bird behavior and migration patterns.
