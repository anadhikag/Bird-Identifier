# Horned Puffin

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Fratercula corniculata  
Family: Alcidae  
Order: Charadriiformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 30–35 cm  
Average Wingspan: 55–65 cm  
Average Weight: 400–600 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a more pronounced black horn-like feather above the eye.  
Distinctive Features: Black and white plumage, yellow beak with colorful patterns, and a tuft of black feathers (in males).  

---

## Habitat  
Breeds on coastal cliffs, rocky islands, and grassy slopes near the sea. Non-breeding individuals are pelagic, spending time in open ocean.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Found along the North Pacific, including Alaska (USA), Russia, and Japan. Breeding colonies are concentrated in these regions.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Migrates from breeding grounds to open ocean habitats in winter, with some populations moving southward.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily feeds on fish (e.g., capelin, herring), crustaceans, and squid. Uses its wings to dive and catch prey underwater.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and gregarious, often seen in groups. Dives to depths of up to 60 meters. Nests in burrows or under rocks to protect eggs from predators.  

---

## Vocalization  
Emits grunting, croaking, and low-pitched calls, especially during courtship and territorial displays.  

---

## Breeding  
Lays one egg per clutch. Both parents share incubation duties, which lasts approximately 45–50 days. Chicks fledge at 5–6 weeks.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Oil spills, climate change disrupting prey availability, and human disturbance at breeding sites.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Acts as a predator of small fish and invertebrates, maintaining marine ecosystem balance. Serves as an indicator species for ocean health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Males develop a prominent horn-like feather during breeding season, which is shed afterward.  
- Capable of flying at speeds up to 55 km/h while foraging.  
- Uses its wings to "fly" underwater, similar to penguins, to pursue prey.
