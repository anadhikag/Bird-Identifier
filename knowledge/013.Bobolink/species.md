# Bobolink

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Dolichonyx oryzivorus  
Family: Icteridae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 15–17 cm  
Average Wingspan: 25–30 cm  
Average Weight: 20–25 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have black and gold plumage with a white belly; females are brown with streaks.  
Distinctive Features: Males display a striking black head and back with a white belly; females resemble sparrows.  

---

## Habitat  
Grasslands, meadows, and agricultural fields.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in North America (central/eastern United States and southern Canada). Winters in South America (Argentina, Uruguay, and Brazil).  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling from North to South America.  

---

## Diet  
Seeds and insects; forages on the ground.  

---

## Behaviour  
Social and gregarious, often seen in flocks outside the breeding season. Males perform aerial displays during courtship.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song consists of rapid, melodic notes; flight call is a sharp "tik."  

---

## Breeding  
Ground-nesting species; clutch size is 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 11–13 days, with fledging occurring 10–12 days post-hatching.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to agricultural intensification and pesticide use.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and serves as an indicator of grassland health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Performs elaborate courtship displays, including aerial "sky-dancing."  
- Migrates over 12,000 km annually, one of the longest migrations of any songbird.  
- Known for its melodic, whistling song that mimics a musical scale.
