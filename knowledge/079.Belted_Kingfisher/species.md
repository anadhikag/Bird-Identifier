# Belted Kingfisher

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Megaceryle alcyon  
Family: Cerylidae  
Order: Coraciiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 28–34 cm  
Average Wingspan: 40–45 cm  
Average Weight: 140–200 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a solid blue belt across the lower breast; females have a chestnut-colored belly band.  
Distinctive Features: Shaggy crest, blue and white plumage, black and white head pattern.  

---

## Habitat  
Rivers, streams, lakes, and coastal areas with vertical perches and exposed banks or cliffs for nesting.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Found across North America, Central America, and northern South America. Breeds in the United States and Canada; winters in the southern U.S., Mexico, and northern South America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate southward in winter; some remain in milder climates.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily fish, supplemented by crustaceans, amphibians, and insects. Hunts by hovering and diving.  

---

## Behaviour  
Solitary and territorial. Often hovers above water before plunging for prey. May forage from perches or walk along the water’s edge.  

---

## Vocalization  
Loud, rattling "kek-kek-kek" call, often repeated in rapid succession.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in burrows dug into riverbanks or cliffs. Clutch size: 5–8 eggs. Incubation lasts 21–23 days, shared by both parents.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, water pollution, and degradation of aquatic ecosystems.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates populations of fish and aquatic invertebrates. Indicator species for healthy waterways.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Can hover motionless for minutes while scanning for prey.  
- Stores excess food in its nest for later consumption.  
- Uses wings to stabilize itself during high-speed dives into water.
