# Summer Tanager

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Piranga rubra  
Family: Thraupidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 6.7 inches (17 cm)  
Average Wingspan: 11 inches (28 cm)  
Average Weight: 0.9 ounces (25 grams)  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are uniformly bright red; females are yellow-green with no streaking.  
Distinctive Features: Males exhibit vivid red plumage, while females have a yellow-green body with a faintly streaked breast.  

---

## Habitat  
Summer Tanagers inhabit open deciduous forests, woodlands, and forest edges, often near water sources.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in the eastern United States from the Great Lakes to the Gulf Coast; winters in northern South America and Central America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Neotropical migrant  
Migration Pattern: Travels from North America to northern South America, with some populations wintering in Central America.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily insectivorous, feeding on bees, wasps, beetles, and caterpillars. Also consumes fruits and berries seasonally.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active and acrobatic foragers, often seen gleaning insects from canopy foliage. Males sing from exposed perches to defend territory.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song consists of a series of varied, musical whistles and warbles; calls include sharp, metallic "chink" notes.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in trees or shrubs, building a cup-shaped nest of twigs and leaves. Clutch size is 3–4 eggs, incubated solely by the female for 12–14 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss in breeding and wintering grounds, pesticide use affecting insect populations.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations, particularly bees and wasps. Seed dispersal occurs through fruit consumption.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Males derive their red plumage from carotenoid pigments in their diet.  
- They consume bees and wasps by removing stingers before ingestion.  
- Nestlings are fed insects regurgitated by parents.
