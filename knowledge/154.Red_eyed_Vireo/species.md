# Red-eyed Vireo

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Vireo olivaceus  
Family: Vireonidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 14–15 cm  
Average Wingspan: 23–25 cm  
Average Weight: 14–18 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have brighter red eyes; otherwise, plumage is similar.  
Distinctive Features: Olive-green upperparts, yellow underparts, two white wing bars, and prominent red eyes.  

---

## Habitat  
Deciduous and mixed forests, especially near water sources. Prefers dense foliage for nesting.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across eastern North America from southern Canada to the Gulf Coast. Winters in northern South America (Colombia to Peru).  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance, traveling from North to South America via Central America.  

---

## Diet  
Insects (caterpillars, beetles, flies) during breeding season; fruits and berries in winter.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active and vocal, often seen flitting through trees. Territorial during breeding; may mob predators.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of short, whistled phrases, often described as "Here, I-sit-on-my-throne." Call: Sharp, high-pitched "see-see."  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in trees or shrubs; cup-shaped nest made of twigs, moss, and lichen. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation: 12–14 days by both parents.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss in breeding and wintering grounds; pesticide use.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and disperses seeds via fruit consumption.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Can sing over 20,000 times daily during breeding season.  
- Holds its bill open while perching to regulate body temperature.  
- Some populations are year-round residents in parts of Central America.
