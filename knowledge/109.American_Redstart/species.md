# American Redstart

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Setophaga ruticilla  
Family: Parulidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 11–13 cm  
Average Wingspan: 20–23 cm  
Average Weight: 9–14 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have black and orange plumage with white wing bars; females are yellow with gray and black markings.  
Distinctive Features: Bright orange patches on the sides and tail, black head and back in males; females have a more subdued yellow and black pattern.  

---

## Habitat  
Deciduous and mixed forests, especially near water sources such as streams or swamps.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in eastern North America, from southern Canada to the northeastern United States. Winters in the Caribbean, northern South America, and parts of Central America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migration from North America to the Caribbean and northern South America.  

---

## Diet  
Insects, including caterpillars, beetles, flies, and spiders. Occasionally consumes small fruits during migration.  

---

## Behaviour  
Active and acrobatic, flitting through branches while foraging. Known for flicking wings and tail to startle prey.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A series of high-pitched, musical notes ("sweet-sweet-sweet-sweet"). Call: A sharp "tsee" or "chip".  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in trees, typically 1–5 meters above ground. Clutch size: 3–4 eggs. Incubation period: 12–14 days, primarily by the female.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss in breeding and wintering grounds, pesticide use.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations, particularly in forest ecosystems. Acts as an indicator species for forest health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Males perform elaborate courtship displays with wing and tail flicks.  
- Known for its striking color contrast during breeding season.  
- Frequently visits backyard feeders in some regions during migration.
