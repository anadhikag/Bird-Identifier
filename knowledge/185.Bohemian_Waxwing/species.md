# Bohemian Waxwing

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Bombycilla garrulus  
Family: Bombycillidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 18–22 cm  
Average Wingspan: 30–35 cm  
Average Weight: 30–45 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are slightly larger and have a longer tail; otherwise, plumage is similar.  
Distinctive Features: Black mask, yellow-tipped tail, and wax-like red-tipped wing feathers (waxwings).  

---

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, coniferous or mixed forests, orchards, and urban areas with fruiting trees.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in boreal and temperate regions of northern Europe and Asia; winters in central Europe, northern Africa, and occasionally North America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Irregular, nomadic movements based on food availability and seasonal changes.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily fruits and berries (e.g., rowan, hawthorn, juniper); insects during the breeding season.  

---

## Behaviour  
Highly social, traveling in flocks. Known for acrobatic flight and flocking behavior. May crash into objects while feeding.  

---

## Vocalization  
Soft, high-pitched calls; song consists of a series of whistles and trills.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in coniferous trees; clutch size 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–15 days, primarily by the female. Both parents feed nestlings.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pesticide use, and climate change affecting food availability.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Seed dispersal through fruit consumption; helps regenerate forest ecosystems. Controls insect populations during breeding.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- The "wax" on their wing feathers is a natural keratin structure, not wax.  
- Nomadic migrations can lead to sudden, large flocks appearing in unexpected regions.  
- Known to consume fermented berries, occasionally leading to intoxicated behavior.
