# Loggerhead Shrike  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Lanius ludovicianus  
Family: Laniidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 20–24 cm  
Average Wingspan: 33–39 cm  
Average Weight: 35–50 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a black facial mask; females may have a fainter mask or none.  
Distinctive Features: Black mask, gray back, white underparts, and a hooked bill.  

---  

## Habitat  
Open habitats such as grasslands, agricultural fields, shrublands, and suburban areas with perches like fence posts or wires.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across central and southern Canada, the United States, and northern Mexico. Winters in the southern U.S., Mexico, and the Caribbean.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate southward in autumn; some populations are resident in milder climates.  

---  

## Diet  
Insects (especially grasshoppers and beetles), small vertebrates (lizards, frogs), and occasionally roadkill. Known to impale prey on thorns or barbed wire.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Solitary or in pairs. Perches on wires or branches to scan for prey. Uses thorns or wires to store food for later consumption.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Call: Sharp, metallic "pee-yep." Song: Varied, includes mimicry of other birds and mechanical sounds.  

---  

## Breeding  
Builds a cup-shaped nest in thorny shrubs or small trees. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 14–17 days, shared by both parents.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pesticide use, and collisions with vehicles.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and scavenges roadkill, reducing disease spread.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- The only North American shrike with a black facial mask.  
- Stores food on thorns or wires to soften it before eating.  
- Mimics calls of other birds and mechanical noises.
