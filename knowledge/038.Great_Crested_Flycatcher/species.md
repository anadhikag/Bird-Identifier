# Great Crested Flycatcher  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Myiarchus crinitus  
Family: Tyrannidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 23 cm  
Average Wingspan: 38 cm  
Average Weight: 34 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males and females are similar in appearance, though males may have slightly more pronounced crests.  
Distinctive Features: Large, prominent crest; olive-brown upperparts; pale yellow underparts; dark eye stripe.  

---  

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, forest edges, suburban areas, and riparian zones.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across central and eastern North America; winters in Central America, northern South America, and the Caribbean.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Breeds in North America (April–September), then migrates to Central and northern South America for winter.  

---  

## Diet  
Insects (especially beetles, grasshoppers, and caterpillars), spiders, and small fruits.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Territorial and solitary; perches prominently to scan for prey. Uses its crest to signal aggression or courtship.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Loud, whistled "kreep" call; a sharp "pip" during flight.  

---  

## Breeding  
Nests in tree cavities or artificial structures; clutch size 2–5 eggs. Incubation lasts 14–16 days, shared by both parents.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pesticide use, and nest-site competition with invasive species.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and disperses seeds through fruit consumption.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- Mimics the posture of a snake to deter predators.  
- Collects bright blue objects (e.g., bottle caps) to decorate nests.  
- Often perches on exposed branches to survey its territory.
