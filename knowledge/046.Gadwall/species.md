# Gadwall

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Anas strepera  
Family: Anatidae  
Order: Anseriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 43–48 cm  
Average Wingspan: 76–85 cm  
Average Weight: 0.8–1.2 kg  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a gray body with a black rear end and a white speculum; females are mottled brown with a pale face and dark eye ring.  
Distinctive Features: Males display a gray plumage with a white wing patch (speculum); females have a pale facial ring and dark crown.  

---

## Habitat  
Freshwater wetlands, marshes, lakes, and rivers with dense vegetation.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across Europe, Asia, and North America; winters in Africa, southern Asia, and the southern United States.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate to warmer regions in winter, often traveling in flocks.  

---

## Diet  
Aquatic plants, seeds, insects, small invertebrates, and algae.  

---

## Behaviour  
Dabbling ducks that feed by tipping forward in water; social and often found in flocks outside breeding season.  

---

## Vocalization  
Males produce soft, low quacks; females emit a quieter, more nasal call compared to other ducks.  

---

## Breeding  
Breeding Season: Spring  
Nest Description: Shallow depression lined with grass and down, built on the ground near water.  
Clutch Size: 8–12 eggs  
Incubation Period: 23–25 days  
Lifespan: Unknown  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, wetland degradation, and hunting.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Seeds dispersal through consumption and excretion; contributes to nutrient cycling in wetland ecosystems.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Hybridizes frequently with mallards, producing fertile offspring.  
- Flies in tight, coordinated flocks during migration.  
- Uses feet to dig for food in soft mud.
