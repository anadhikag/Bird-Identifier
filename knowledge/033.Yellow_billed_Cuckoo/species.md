# Yellow-billed Cuckoo

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Coccyzus americanus  
Family: Cuculidae  
Order: Cuculiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 28–33 cm  
Average Wingspan: 46–51 cm  
Average Weight: 45–65 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are slightly larger and may have more pronounced black markings.  
Distinctive Features: Slender body with a long tail, black and white plumage, and a distinctive yellow bill.  

---

## Habitat  
Open woodlands, riparian areas, and forests with dense undergrowth. Prefers areas near water sources.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds in North America from southern Canada to northern Mexico; winters in Central and South America. Key breeding regions include the southwestern United States and parts of Mexico.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migration from North America to Central and South America, traveling through Central America and the Caribbean.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily insects, including caterpillars, beetles, grasshoppers, and spiders. Occasionally consumes small vertebrates.  

---

## Behaviour  
Solitary and territorial during breeding. Known for loud, persistent calls and mobbing predators. Often perches on branches while foraging.  

---

## Vocalization  
Distinctive "ka-ka-ka" vocalizations, often repeated in rapid succession. Calls are used for communication and territory defense.  

---

## Breeding  
Builds flimsy, open-cup nests in trees or shrubs. Clutch size is 2–4 eggs. Incubation lasts 10–12 days, shared by both parents. Chicks fledge after 10–12 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, particularly in riparian zones, and pesticide use reducing insect populations.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Helps control insect populations, particularly during outbreaks of caterpillars. Indicates ecosystem health due to reliance on diverse insect prey.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Known for eating large numbers of caterpillars, including toxic species like monarchs.  
- Migrates at night, using celestial cues for navigation.  
- Has a unique "horse-like" call that can be mistaken for a small mammal.
