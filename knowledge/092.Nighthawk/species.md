# Common Nighthawk

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Chordeiles minor  
Family: Caprimulgidae  
Order: Caprimulgiformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 22–24 cm  
Average Wingspan: 51–56 cm  
Average Weight: 55–85 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a slightly more prominent white throat patch.  
Distinctive Features: Mottled brown and gray plumage, white wing bars, and a "broken" white throat patch.  

---

## Habitat  
Open habitats including fields, deserts, urban areas, and forest edges. Nests on bare ground or gravel.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across North America from southern Canada to northern Mexico. Winters in South America, primarily in Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migrant; travels from North America to South America, often crossing the Gulf of Mexico.  

---

## Diet  
Insects, primarily moths, beetles, and flies, caught in flight using their large, gaping mouths.  

---

## Behaviour  
Crepuscular (active at dawn and dusk). Performs aerial courtship displays with U-shaped dives and loud "booming" sounds.  

---

## Vocalization  
Distinctive "peent" call during flight displays. Also emits soft, clucking sounds during courtship.  

---

## Breeding  
Lays 1–3 camouflaged eggs directly on open ground. Incubation lasts 18–20 days, shared by both parents. Chicks fledge at 19–22 days.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, pesticide use reducing insect prey, and collisions with vehicles/structures.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations, particularly nocturnal species. Serves as an indicator of ecosystem health due to reliance on insect abundance.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Performs dramatic "booming" dives during mating displays.  
- Eggs are highly camouflaged to blend with gravel or bare soil.  
- Mistaken for moths due to mottled plumage and erratic flight.
