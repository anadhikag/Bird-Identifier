# Chipping Sparrow

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Spizella passerina  
Family: Passerellidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 12–14 cm  
Average Wingspan: 18–21 cm  
Average Weight: 12–18 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males have a darker crown and more distinct supercilium (eyestripe) than females.  
Distinctive Features: Black eyestripe, white throat, and a short conical bill.  

---

## Habitat  
Open grasslands, meadows, agricultural fields, and urban parks.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across North America from southern Canada to northern Mexico; winters in the southern U.S., Mexico, and Central America.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Long-distance migrants traveling from breeding grounds in North America to wintering areas in the southern U.S. and Central America.  

---

## Diet  
Insects (especially grasshoppers and beetles) during breeding season; seeds and grains in winter.  

---

## Behaviour  
Ground-foraging birds that often feed in flocks outside the breeding season. Display aerial displays during courtship.  

---

## Vocalization  
Song: A rapid, trilling "tsee-tsee-tsee" or a series of short, high-pitched notes. Call: Sharp "tik" or "chip."  

---

## Breeding  
Nests on the ground in grassy areas. Clutch size: 3–5 eggs. Incubation: 12–14 days, primarily by the female.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss from agricultural expansion and urbanization.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations and aids in seed dispersal.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- One of the most common sparrows in North America.  
- Their song is a staple in birding contests and recordings.  
- Historically kept as cage birds in parts of Asia due to their melodic calls.
