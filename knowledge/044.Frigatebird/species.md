# Magnificent Frigatebird

## Basic Information
Scientific Name: Fregata magnificens  
Family: Fregatidae  
Order: Suliformes  

---

## Identification
Average Length: 85–100 cm  
Average Wingspan: 200–210 cm  
Average Weight: 1.2–1.5 kg  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are uniformly black with a red gular pouch; females have a dark back, white underparts, and a smaller gular pouch.  
Distinctive Features: Long, narrow wings; deeply forked tail; males possess an inflatable red throat pouch used in courtship displays.  

---

## Habitat  
Coastal and oceanic environments, including tropical islands, mangroves, and open seas.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
Widespread in tropical regions of the Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans. Breeds in the Caribbean, Gulf of Mexico, West Africa, and along the coasts of Southeast Asia.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Transoceanic movements, following warm currents and food availability. Non-breeding populations travel vast distances across open oceans.  

---

## Diet  
Primarily fish (e.g., flying fish), squid, and marine invertebrates. Known for kleptoparasitism, stealing food from other seabirds mid-air.  

---

## Behaviour  
Highly aerial, capable of soaring for weeks without landing. Social and colonial, often seen in groups. Uses gular pouch in dramatic courtship displays.  

---

## Vocalization  
Loud, harsh calls, especially during breeding season. Males produce booming sounds by inflating their gular pouch.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in colonies on islands or coastal trees. Females lay a single egg, incubated by both parents for ~50 days. Chicks fledge after 4–5 months.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, plastic pollution, and human disturbance on nesting sites.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates marine prey populations and serves as an indicator of ocean health.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Can fly continuously for weeks without landing.  
- Males use their gular pouch to perform elaborate aerial displays during courtship.  
- Known for "pirating" food from other seabirds, such as boobies and terns.
