# Brown Creeper

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Certhia americana  
Family: Certhiidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---

## Identification  
Average Length: 13–15 cm  
Average Wingspan: 20–23 cm  
Average Weight: 14–20 g  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are slightly larger and may have a more pronounced white eyebrow stripe.  
Distinctive Features: Brown upperparts with faint streaks, pale underparts, a long, decurved bill, and a white eye ring.  

---

## Habitat  
Coniferous and mixed forests, especially areas with mature trees for climbing and foraging.  

---

## Geographic Distribution  
North America, ranging from southern Canada to the northern United States.  

---

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Partially migratory  
Migration Pattern: Northern populations migrate southward in winter; some remain year-round in milder regions.  

---

## Diet  
Insects, spiders, and other small invertebrates found under tree bark.  

---

## Behaviour  
Climbs trees spirally using stiff tail feathers for support, often remaining motionless to avoid detection. Solitary and non-social outside breeding season.  

---

## Vocalization  
A soft, high-pitched "fee-bee" call and a thin, whistled "psee" note.  

---

## Breeding  
Nests in tree crevices or under loose bark, lined with moss and lichen. Clutch size: 4–6 eggs. Incubation lasts 12–14 days, primarily by the female.  

---

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to deforestation and forest fragmentation.  

---

## Ecological Importance  
Controls insect populations by feeding on bark-dwelling pests. Their nesting habits contribute to tree cavity ecosystems.  

---

## Interesting Facts  
- Climbs trees using a specialized technique, relying on stiff tail feathers for balance.  
- Camouflages itself with bark-like plumage to avoid predators.  
- Often overlooked due to its cryptic behavior and coloration.
