# Blue Jay  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Cyanocitta cristata  
Family: Corvidae  
Order: Passeriformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 22–30 cm  
Average Wingspan: 34–42 cm  
Average Weight: 70–100 g  
Male vs Female Differences: No significant visual differences; both exhibit similar plumage and size.  
Distinctive Features: Bright blue upperparts, black markings on the throat and chest, white underparts, and a prominent blue crest.  

---  

## Habitat  
Deciduous and mixed forests, woodlands, suburban gardens, and parks.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Eastern and central North America, from southern Canada to the Gulf Coast of the United States.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Non-migratory  
Migration Pattern: Sedentary; may move short distances for food or weather.  

---  

## Diet  
Omnivorous; feeds on nuts, seeds, insects, fruits, eggs, and small animals.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Highly intelligent and social; forms family groups, caches food, and exhibits problem-solving skills.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Loud, variable calls including harsh "jay" sounds, mimicry of other birds, and alarm cries.  

---  

## Breeding  
Nests in trees; 2–7 eggs laid in spring. Incubation lasts 17–18 days, primarily by the female.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss, vehicle collisions, and pesticide exposure.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Seed disperser; aids in forest regeneration. Controls insect populations by preying on larvae and beetles.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- Mimics calls of hawks and other birds to deter predators or competitors.  
- Stores food in hidden caches, often forgetting some, which aids in seed dispersal.  
- Known for strong family bonds, with offspring sometimes helping parents raise younger siblings.
