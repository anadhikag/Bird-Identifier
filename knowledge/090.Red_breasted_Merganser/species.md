# Red-breasted Merganser  

## Basic Information  
Scientific Name: Mergus serrator  
Family: Anatidae  
Order: Anseriformes  

---  

## Identification  
Average Length: 50–65 cm  
Average Wingspan: 85–100 cm  
Average Weight: 1.2–2.3 kg  
Male vs Female Differences: Males are slightly larger and have a more vibrant red-brown head; females have a paler head and neck.  
Distinctive Features: Serrated bill edges for gripping fish, red-brown head with a white neck ring, and dark back.  

---  

## Habitat  
Coastal marine environments, rivers, lakes, and estuaries. Prefers fast-moving water for feeding.  

---  

## Geographic Distribution  
Breeds across the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America, Europe, and Asia. Winters in temperate coastal zones, including the Atlantic and Pacific coasts of North America, Europe, and Japan.  

---  

## Migration  
Migratory Status: Migratory  
Migration Pattern: Travels from Arctic breeding grounds to temperate coasts for winter, often in large flocks.  

---  

## Diet  
Primarily fish-eating (piscivorous), with a diet of small fish like herring and smelt. Also consumes crustaceans and aquatic insects.  

---  

## Behaviour  
Active divers and agile swimmers, using their serrated bills to catch prey. Often forages in groups. Non-breeding flocks gather in coastal bays and estuaries.  

---  

## Vocalization  
Soft grunts and whistles during social interactions. Males produce low, hoarse calls; females emit higher-pitched sounds.  

---  

## Breeding  
Nests in ground depressions near water, often on islands or in dense vegetation. Clutch size: 6–10 eggs. Incubation period: 28–30 days, primarily by the female.  

---  

## Conservation  
IUCN Status: Least Concern  
Threats: Habitat loss due to coastal development, pollution, and oil spills.  

---  

## Ecological Importance  
Regulates fish populations in aquatic ecosystems. Serves as an indicator species for wetland health.  

---  

## Interesting Facts  
- Their serrated bill edges help grip slippery fish during feeding.  
- Red-breasted Mergansers are among the most agile divers among waterfowl.  
- They often form large, mixed-species flocks during migration.
